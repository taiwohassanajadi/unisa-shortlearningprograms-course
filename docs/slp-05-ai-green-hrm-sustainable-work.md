# SLP 05: AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HRM practitioners, sustainability managers, ESG teams, organisational development practitioners, learning and development teams, public sector managers, consultants and postgraduate learners interested in sustainable digital work.

## Problem Being Solved

AI adoption is often treated as a productivity issue, while sustainability is treated as a separate ESG issue. In practice, organisations need people systems that support both: green skills, sustainable work practices, responsible digital adoption and awareness of AI's environmental footprint.

This SLP connects **Green HRM** with AI-enabled work transformation. It teaches learners how HR can support sustainable behaviour, digital work redesign and responsible AI choices.

## Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to design HRM practices that support sustainable work transformation in AI-enabled organisations.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion, learners should be able to:

- Explain Green HRM and its relevance to AI-enabled workplaces.
- Identify sustainability implications of AI adoption and digital work.
- Design HR practices that support green skills, employee involvement and sustainable work behaviour.
- Evaluate AI-enabled work redesign through productivity, inclusion and sustainability lenses.
- Produce a sustainable AI + HRM implementation plan.

## Study Units

### AHRM05A - Green HRM and Sustainable Work

Content areas:

- Green HRM foundations.
- HRM practices for environmental management: recruitment, training, performance, rewards and employee involvement.
- Green skills and sustainability capability.
- Employee participation in sustainability initiatives.
- Sustainable work and organisational culture.

### AHRM05B - AI, Digital Work and Environmental Impact

Content areas:

- Digital transformation and sustainability.
- Environmental footprint of AI systems.
- Green AI and sustainable AI distinctions.
- Choosing appropriate AI tools for the task.
- Remote work, hybrid work and digital collaboration trade-offs.
- Responsible procurement and vendor questions.

### AHRM05C - Redesigning Work for Sustainable Performance

Content areas:

- Work redesign for productivity, wellbeing and sustainability.
- AI-supported learning and skills development.
- Reducing waste in HR processes.
- Sustainable employee experience.
- Inclusion and access in digital work.
- Change management for sustainable behaviour.

### AHRM05D - Implementation, Metrics and Reporting

Content areas:

- Sustainable HRM metrics.
- Green skills development plans.
- Employee involvement and feedback loops.
- AI use metrics linked to cost, value and environmental awareness.
- ESG and HR reporting alignment.
- Building a sustainable AI + HRM roadmap.

## Assessment

- **Formative task 1:** Green HRM practice audit.
- **Formative task 2:** Sustainable AI use-case review.
- **Formative task 3:** Green skills and employee involvement plan.
- **Final portfolio:** Sustainable AI + HRM transformation plan.

## Capstone Artefact

Learners produce a **Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan** containing:

- Organisational context.
- Current Green HRM practice audit.
- Selected AI-enabled work process.
- Sustainability risks and opportunities.
- Green skills plan.
- Employee involvement strategy.
- Metrics and implementation roadmap.

## Evidence Base

Green HRM literature shows that HR practices can support environmental management through employee capability, motivation and involvement. Green AI literature adds a newer concern: AI systems themselves have environmental costs and should be selected, governed and measured with sustainability in mind. This SLP combines both strands for a practical management audience.
