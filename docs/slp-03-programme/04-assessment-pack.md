# Assessment Pack and Rubrics

## Assessment Overview

The course uses an applied decision-brief portfolio model. Each formative task builds toward the final People Analytics Decision Brief.

| Assessment | Weight | Due | Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Formative Task 1 | 10% | Week 3 | HR decision question and analytics framing memo |
| Formative Task 2 | 15% | Week 6 | Data quality and risk review |
| Formative Task 3 | 15% | Week 8 | AI-assisted analysis interpretation task |
| Final Portfolio | 60% | Week 12 | People Analytics Decision Brief |

## Formative Task 1: HR Decision Question and Analytics Framing Memo

### Purpose

Learners define a workplace or HRM decision question before selecting metrics or tools.

### Instructions

Select one HRM or workforce issue. Examples include turnover, absenteeism, recruitment quality, onboarding, learning impact, employee engagement, skills gaps or workload distribution.

Submit a 1,000 to 1,200 word memo covering:

- Workplace context.
- Decision that needs support.
- Main analytics question.
- Stakeholders.
- Possible data sources.
- Initial metrics.
- Why the question matters.
- Risks of poor interpretation.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Decision question clarity | 30% |
| Workplace relevance | 20% |
| Data source and metric logic | 20% |
| Stakeholder and interpretation-risk awareness | 20% |
| Clarity and professionalism | 10% |

## Formative Task 2: Data Quality and Risk Review

### Purpose

Learners evaluate whether available people data is suitable for decision support.

### Instructions

Submit a data quality and risk review for your selected decision question. Include:

- Data source map.
- Metric definitions.
- Data quality concerns.
- Missing data issues.
- Validity and reliability concerns.
- POPIA and confidentiality issues.
- Bias and fairness risks.
- Limitations of interpretation.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Data source and metric definition quality | 20% |
| Data quality analysis | 25% |
| POPIA and confidentiality awareness | 20% |
| Bias and fairness risk analysis | 20% |
| Interpretation limits and clarity | 15% |

## Formative Task 3: AI-Assisted Analysis Interpretation Task

### Purpose

Learners practise interpreting AI-assisted or simulated AI-assisted analytics outputs responsibly.

### Instructions

Use a fictional or anonymised analytics scenario. Submit:

- The decision question.
- A short dataset summary or dashboard description.
- The AI prompt or simulated AI interpretation.
- A critique of the AI-assisted interpretation.
- Corrected interpretation.
- Recommended management action.
- Uncertainty and caution notes.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Appropriate AI-assisted analysis use | 20% |
| Quality of interpretation critique | 25% |
| Evidence-informed corrected interpretation | 25% |
| Responsible recommendation | 20% |
| Professional structure | 10% |

## Final Portfolio: People Analytics Decision Brief

### Purpose

Learners integrate the course learning into a practical decision brief for one HRM or workforce issue.

### Required Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Workplace context and decision problem.
3. Analytics question.
4. Stakeholder map.
5. Data source map.
6. Metric definitions.
7. Data quality and risk review.
8. Analysis and interpretation.
9. AI-assisted interpretation notes.
10. POPIA, ethics and fairness considerations.
11. Recommended action.
12. Evaluation plan.
13. Limitations and next steps.

Recommended length: 4,000 to 5,500 words.

### Final Portfolio Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Decision problem and analytics question | 10% |
| Data source and metric quality | 15% |
| Data quality, POPIA and risk review | 15% |
| Analysis and interpretation | 15% |
| AI-assisted output critique | 10% |
| Ethics, fairness and accountability | 10% |
| Recommendation and evaluation plan | 15% |
| Professional structure and communication | 10% |

## AI Use in Assessment

Learners may use AI tools for brainstorming, structuring questions, summarising fictional data and drafting narratives, but they must:

- Disclose how AI was used.
- Avoid uploading personal, candidate, employee or confidential organisational information.
- Verify AI-assisted interpretation against the evidence.
- State uncertainty and limitations.
- Take responsibility for final recommendations.
