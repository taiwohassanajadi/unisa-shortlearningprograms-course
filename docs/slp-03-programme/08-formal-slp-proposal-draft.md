# Formal SLP Proposal Draft

## Programme Title

**People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**

## Host College, Department and Centres

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Motivation

Organisations increasingly collect people data through HR systems, surveys, learning platforms, recruitment tools, performance processes and digital work systems. Yet many managers still struggle to convert people data into trustworthy decisions. AI tools can support analysis and interpretation, but they also introduce risks when data is weak, privacy is ignored, bias is hidden or recommendations are treated as more certain than they are.

This SLP responds to the need for practical, responsible and evidence-informed people analytics capability. It is suitable for HR managers, analysts, departmental heads, workforce planners, auditors and postgraduate learners who need to use people data without losing sight of fairness, privacy, accountability and managerial judgment.

## Target Group

The programme is aimed at:

- HR managers.
- HR analysts.
- Departmental heads.
- Workforce planners.
- Talent managers.
- Organisational development practitioners.
- Internal auditors and compliance practitioners.
- Consultants.
- Postgraduate learners and researchers.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to use people analytics and AI-assisted decision support in HRM while maintaining evidence quality, fairness, transparency, privacy and managerial accountability.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

Learners who complete the programme should be able to:

1. Formulate HRM and workforce decision questions that can be answered with people data.
2. Distinguish descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics.
3. Evaluate the quality, limits and risks of HR data and analytics outputs.
4. Use AI tools to support analysis while checking assumptions, bias, uncertainty and interpretation.
5. Produce a people analytics decision-support brief for a selected workforce issue.

## Programme Duration

12 weeks.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Language Medium

English.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have basic spreadsheet and digital literacy and an interest in HRM, auditing, management, workforce planning, organisational development or evidence-based decision-making. Coding knowledge is not required.

## Programme Content

### Module 1: Foundations of People Analytics

Purpose: To introduce learners to people analytics as decision support.

Areas covered:

- People analytics and evidence-based HRM.
- HR metrics versus decisions.
- Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics.
- Common workforce questions.
- Analytics as a managerial capability.

### Module 2: Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation

Purpose: To teach learners how to evaluate people data before using it for decisions.

Areas covered:

- HR data sources.
- Metric definitions.
- Validity, reliability, missing data and consistency.
- Correlation, causation and uncertainty.
- Bias and privacy risks.

### Module 3: AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support

Purpose: To teach learners how to use AI tools to support analysis without surrendering judgment.

Areas covered:

- AI-assisted question structuring.
- Summarising qualitative feedback.
- Dashboard interpretation.
- Narrative reporting.
- AI output critique.
- Human decision rights and accountability.

### Module 4: Analytics Governance, Action and Evaluation

Purpose: To enable learners to translate analytics into responsible and measurable workplace action.

Areas covered:

- POPIA and employee data governance.
- Analytics governance roles.
- Insight-to-action planning.
- Intervention design.
- Evaluation and maturity roadmap.
- Audit evidence for analytics decisions.

## Assessment

The programme uses a portfolio-based assessment model:

- Formative task 1: HR decision question and analytics framing memo, 10%.
- Formative task 2: Data quality and risk review, 15%.
- Formative task 3: AI-assisted analysis interpretation task, 15%.
- Final portfolio: People Analytics Decision Brief, 60%.

## Final Portfolio

The final portfolio requires learners to prepare a decision brief for one HRM or workforce issue. It includes:

- Decision problem.
- Analytics question.
- Data source map.
- Metric definitions.
- Data quality review.
- AI-assisted interpretation critique.
- POPIA, ethics and fairness considerations.
- Recommendation.
- Evaluation plan.

The programme is informed by HR analytics scholarship, NIST AI RMF, POPIA resources, OECD AI Principles, ISO human capital reporting guidance, ISO/IEC 42001 and practitioner people analytics resources.
