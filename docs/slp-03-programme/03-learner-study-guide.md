# Learner Study Guide

## Welcome

Welcome to **People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**. This programme is for learners who want to use people data more responsibly and more usefully in HRM and workforce decisions.

The course is not a data science bootcamp. It is a management and governance course. You will learn how to ask better HR questions, examine data quality, interpret AI-supported analysis and communicate recommendations without overstating what the data proves.

## How to Use This Study Guide

Each week you should:

1. Read the weekly study notes.
2. Complete the analytics activity.
3. Add evidence to your final decision brief.
4. Record uncertainty, assumptions and limitations.
5. Participate in the discussion or peer review activity.

## Core Principle

People analytics should improve human decisions. It should not replace judgment, ignore context or turn employees into numbers without dignity, privacy or voice.

## Study Unit 1: Foundations of People Analytics

### Key Ideas

People analytics uses workforce data to improve decisions about people, work and organisations. It includes metrics, analysis, interpretation and action. A dashboard is not the same thing as a decision. A metric is not useful unless it is connected to a clear question.

Good people analytics begins with a decision question. For example:

- Why is turnover rising in one department?
- Which onboarding problems are delaying productivity?
- Are learning programmes improving capability?
- Where are skills gaps affecting service delivery?
- Is absenteeism linked to workload, wellbeing or scheduling issues?

### Practical Focus

You will select one HRM or workforce decision question and explain why it matters.

### Reflection Question

What decision are you trying to improve, and what would count as better evidence?

## Study Unit 2: Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation

### Key Ideas

People data can be incomplete, inconsistent, outdated, biased or misunderstood. A high-quality chart can still mislead if the underlying measure is weak. For example, "time to hire" may be easy to calculate but may not show whether the best candidates were selected. "Training attendance" may not show whether people learned anything useful.

Learners should ask:

- What does the metric actually measure?
- What does it leave out?
- Who is included or excluded?
- Is the data accurate and current?
- Could the data reflect historical bias?
- Can the analysis support the conclusion being made?

### Practical Focus

You will review data sources, metric definitions and interpretation risks for your selected decision question.

### Reflection Question

What would make a manager trust this analysis too much, and how can you prevent that?

## Study Unit 3: AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support

### Key Ideas

AI tools can support people analytics by helping structure questions, summarise qualitative feedback, compare options, draft narratives and generate hypotheses. However, AI tools can also produce confident but unsupported interpretations.

Responsible AI-assisted analysis requires:

- Clear instructions.
- Clean and appropriate data.
- No personal or confidential data in public AI tools.
- Verification against source data.
- Human review.
- Clear statement of assumptions and uncertainty.

### Practical Focus

You will interpret a fictional analytics scenario and use AI or simulated AI outputs to draft a decision-support narrative.

### Reflection Question

What part of the analysis should AI support, and what part must remain a human management decision?

## Study Unit 4: Analytics Governance, Action and Evaluation

### Key Ideas

Analytics creates value only when it improves decisions and action. A recommendation should be feasible, ethical and measurable. It should also be governed: managers need to know who owns the data, who reviews the analysis, who approves action and how outcomes will be checked.

People analytics governance includes:

- Data ownership.
- Purpose limitation.
- POPIA and confidentiality.
- Metric definitions.
- Quality review.
- Bias and fairness checks.
- Decision rights.
- Monitoring and evaluation.

### Practical Focus

You will convert your analysis into an action and evaluation plan.

### Reflection Question

How will you know whether the analytics-informed recommendation improved the workplace outcome?

## Final Portfolio

Your final portfolio is a **People Analytics Decision Brief**. It should help a manager understand the problem, the evidence, the limits of the analysis, the recommended action and how to evaluate results.
