# Curriculum and Weekly Learning Matrix

## Weekly Structure

The course is designed for 12 weeks. Each week includes guided study, applied analytics practice, discussion and contribution to the final decision brief.

| Week | Study Unit | Topic | Learning Outcomes | Learning Activities | Assessment Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AHRM03A | Orientation: people analytics as decision support | Explain the course purpose and identify workforce decisions that need evidence | Analytics self-audit, decision-question discussion | Portfolio context note |
| 2 | AHRM03A | HR metrics, evidence-based HRM and decision quality | Distinguish metrics, evidence and managerial decisions | Metric critique, evidence ladder activity | Short reflection |
| 3 | AHRM03A | Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics | Classify analytics types and match them to HR questions | Analytics type sorting, question-framing clinic | Formative task 1 submission |
| 4 | AHRM03B | Data sources, data quality and measurement | Evaluate HR data sources, validity and reliability | Data inventory, quality checklist | Formative task 2 preparation |
| 5 | AHRM03B | Interpretation, causality and uncertainty | Distinguish correlation from causation and communicate uncertainty | Scenario analysis, misleading chart review | Data quality review draft |
| 6 | AHRM03B | Bias, privacy and POPIA in people data | Identify privacy, bias and fairness risks in HR analytics | POPIA review, bias mapping | Formative task 2 submission |
| 7 | AHRM03C | AI-assisted analysis and sensemaking | Use AI to structure questions, summarise feedback and generate hypotheses | AI interpretation lab, output verification | Formative task 3 preparation |
| 8 | AHRM03C | Dashboards, narratives and management recommendations | Convert analytics outputs into decision-ready narratives | Dashboard critique, recommendation memo | Formative task 3 submission |
| 9 | AHRM03D | Analytics governance and decision rights | Define roles, review points and accountability for analytics use | Governance map, decision-rights matrix | Portfolio draft |
| 10 | AHRM03D | Translating insights into interventions | Link findings to action, change management and employee experience | Intervention design workshop | Portfolio draft |
| 11 | AHRM03D | Evaluation and analytics maturity | Define success metrics and review analytics maturity | Evaluation plan, maturity roadmap | Portfolio draft review |
| 12 | Capstone | Final decision brief presentation | Present a defensible people analytics decision brief | Peer presentation, final reflection | Final portfolio submission |

## Study Unit Outcomes

### AHRM03A - Foundations of People Analytics

Learners should be able to:

- Explain people analytics and evidence-based HRM.
- Formulate decision questions before choosing metrics.
- Distinguish metrics, analytics and decisions.
- Match analytics types to HRM problems.

### AHRM03B - Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation

Learners should be able to:

- Identify HR data sources and their limitations.
- Evaluate validity, reliability, completeness and timeliness.
- Interpret analytics outputs without overstating certainty.
- Identify bias, privacy and POPIA risks in people data.

### AHRM03C - AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support

Learners should be able to:

- Use AI tools to structure analysis, summarise qualitative feedback and generate hypotheses.
- Check AI-assisted outputs against evidence and context.
- Interpret dashboards and analytical narratives critically.
- Communicate recommendations responsibly to managers.

### AHRM03D - Analytics Governance, Action and Evaluation

Learners should be able to:

- Define governance roles and decision rights for people analytics.
- Translate insights into practical interventions.
- Evaluate whether analytics-informed action improved outcomes.
- Build a people analytics maturity roadmap.

## Workload Estimate

Recommended weekly workload:

- Guided reading: 2 hours.
- Learning material and recorded lecture: 1 hour.
- Practical analytics activity: 2 hours.
- Discussion or peer review: 1 hour.
- Portfolio development: 1 to 2 hours.

Estimated total workload: 84 to 96 hours.
