# Programme Specification

## Programme Title

**People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Academic Home

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HR managers, HR analysts, departmental heads, workforce planners, talent managers, organisational development practitioners, internal auditors, compliance practitioners, consultants and postgraduate learners who need to use people data and AI-supported analysis for better workplace decisions.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have:

- Basic spreadsheet and digital literacy.
- Interest or experience in HRM, management, workforce planning, auditing, organisational development, analytics or evidence-based decision-making.
- Ability to interpret basic tables, charts and workplace reports.
- No coding or advanced statistics requirement.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to use people analytics and AI-assisted decision support in HRM while maintaining evidence quality, fairness, transparency, privacy and managerial accountability.

## Problem Statement

Many organisations collect large amounts of employee, candidate and workforce data but struggle to convert it into trustworthy decisions. Dashboards and AI tools can create false certainty when measures are weak, data is incomplete, or managers do not understand causality, bias and interpretation.

This SLP teaches learners to frame HRM questions, evaluate data quality, interpret analytics outputs, use AI tools responsibly and produce recommendations that are useful, ethical and evidence-informed.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

On successful completion, learners should be able to:

1. Formulate HRM and workforce decision questions that can be answered with people data.
2. Distinguish descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics.
3. Evaluate the quality, limits and risks of HR data and analytics outputs.
4. Use AI tools to support analysis while checking assumptions, bias, uncertainty and interpretation.
5. Produce a people analytics decision-support brief for a selected workforce issue.

## Programme Structure

The programme has four study units and one capstone portfolio:

- **AHRM03A:** Foundations of People Analytics
- **AHRM03B:** Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation
- **AHRM03C:** AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support
- **AHRM03D:** Analytics Governance, Action and Evaluation
- **Capstone:** People Analytics Decision Brief

## Assessment Structure

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

## Capstone Output

Learners produce a **People Analytics Decision Brief** containing:

- HRM or workforce problem statement.
- Decision question.
- Stakeholder map.
- Data source map.
- Metric definitions.
- Data quality and risk review.
- Analysis approach.
- AI-assisted interpretation notes.
- Ethical and POPIA considerations.
- Recommended action and evaluation plan.

## Graduate Attributes

The programme supports:

- Evidence-informed decision-making.
- Responsible data use.
- Audit-minded review of metrics and evidence.
- Ethical and human-centred analytics.
- Practical communication for managers.
- Critical interpretation of AI-assisted analysis.
