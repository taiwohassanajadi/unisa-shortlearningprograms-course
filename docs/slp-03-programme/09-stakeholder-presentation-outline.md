# Stakeholder Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

**People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**

Subtitle: Proposed Short Learning Programme under CAS, Department of Auditing, Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, and Centre for Lifelong Learning.

## Slide 2: Why This Programme, Why Now

- Organisations collect more employee and workforce data than ever.
- Managers need evidence-informed decisions, not more dashboards.
- AI can support analysis but can also create false confidence.
- People analytics must address privacy, fairness, accountability and action.

## Slide 3: The Problem

People analytics often fails when:

- Questions are vague.
- Metrics are poorly defined.
- Data quality is ignored.
- Correlation is treated as causation.
- AI outputs are accepted uncritically.
- Recommendations are not evaluated.

## Slide 4: Target Learners

- HR managers.
- HR analysts.
- Departmental heads.
- Workforce planners.
- Talent managers.
- Organisational development practitioners.
- Internal auditors and compliance practitioners.
- Consultants and postgraduate learners.

## Slide 5: Programme Promise

Learners will leave with a practical **People Analytics Decision Brief** that can support a real workforce decision.

## Slide 6: Programme Structure

- Week 1 to 3: People analytics foundations and decision framing.
- Week 4 to 6: Data quality, measurement, POPIA and interpretation.
- Week 7 to 8: AI-assisted analysis and decision narratives.
- Week 9 to 11: Governance, action and evaluation.
- Week 12: Final decision brief presentation.

## Slide 7: Four Study Units

1. Foundations of People Analytics.
2. Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation.
3. AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support.
4. Analytics Governance, Action and Evaluation.

## Slide 8: Assessment Model

- HR decision question and analytics framing memo: 10%.
- Data quality and risk review: 15%.
- AI-assisted analysis interpretation task: 15%.
- Final people analytics decision brief: 60%.

## Slide 9: Responsible Analytics and AI

The programme integrates:

- POPIA and employee data governance.
- Data quality and metric definitions.
- Bias and fairness awareness.
- AI output critique.
- Human decision rights.
- Evaluation and audit evidence.

## Slide 10: Why CAS and Department of Auditing

- People analytics requires evidence discipline.
- Metrics need definitions, controls and traceability.
- AI-assisted analysis needs review and audit evidence.
- The Department of Auditing adds a strong assurance and control lens.

This SLP helps organisations move from people data to better, fairer and more accountable workforce decisions.
