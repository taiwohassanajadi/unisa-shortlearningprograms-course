# Reading List and Research Notes

## Review Scope

This reading list supports SLP 03: **People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**. It uses a rapid scoping approach focused on:

- Evidence-based HRM and people analytics.
- HR metrics and decision quality.
- AI-assisted analysis and interpretation.
- Data quality, bias, fairness and POPIA.
- Workforce decision support and analytics governance.

## Required Readings

1. Marler, J. H., & Boudreau, J. W. *An evidence-based review of HR Analytics.*  
   URL: https://doi.org/10.1080/09585192.2016.1244699  
   Course use: Weeks 1 to 3. Establishes that HR analytics should improve decision quality, not merely increase reporting activity.

2. Tambe, P., Cappelli, P., & Yakubovich, V. *Artificial Intelligence in Human Resources Management: Challenges and a Path Forward.*  
   URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/0008125619867910  
   Course use: Weeks 6 to 9. Explains why AI in HRM is difficult because of complex human outcomes, small data, accountability and employee reactions.

3. NIST. *AI Risk Management Framework.*  
   URL: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework  
   Course use: Weeks 6, 9 and 11. Provides a risk-management structure for AI-assisted analysis and decision support.

4. Information Regulator South Africa. POPIA resources.  
   URL: https://inforegulator.org.za/acts/  
   Course use: Week 6. Provides the South African data protection foundation for employee and candidate data.

5. OECD. *AI Principles.*  
   URL: https://oecd.ai/en/ai-principles  
   Course use: Weeks 6 and 9. Supports human-centred, transparent and accountable AI decision support.

6. World Economic Forum. *The Future of Jobs Report 2025.*  
   URL: https://www.weforum.org/publications/the-future-of-jobs-report-2025/  
   Course use: Weeks 1 and 11. Contextualises workforce planning, skills change and analytics needs.

7. ISO. *ISO 30414: Human resource management - Guidelines for internal and external human capital reporting.*  
   URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/69338.html  
   Course use: Weeks 2 and 4. Provides a human-capital reporting reference point for HR metrics and measurement discipline.

8. ISO. *ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Artificial intelligence management system.*  
   URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001  
   Course use: Weeks 9 and 11. Supports governance and management-system thinking for AI-assisted analytics.

## Evidence Synthesis

### People Analytics Must Begin With a Decision

The literature repeatedly distinguishes analytics that improves decisions from reporting that merely produces more numbers. The course therefore begins with decision-question framing before dashboards, metrics or AI tools.

### HR Data Is Often Messier Than It Looks

People data may be incomplete, inconsistent, subjective or historically biased. Performance ratings, exit interviews, engagement surveys and training records all require careful interpretation. The course teaches learners to document data limitations before recommending action.

### AI Can Support Analysis But Can Also Overstate Certainty

AI tools can help structure questions, summarise qualitative comments and draft narratives, but they can also generate unsupported explanations. The course requires learners to critique AI-assisted interpretations and distinguish evidence from speculation.

### POPIA and Trust Are Central to People Analytics

People analytics relies on employee and candidate information. Data use must be lawful, proportionate, secure and transparent. Even technically valid analytics can damage trust if employees experience it as surveillance or unfair profiling.

### Audit Thinking Strengthens Analytics Governance

The Department of Auditing lens adds value because analytics decisions require traceable data definitions, quality checks, evidence trails, control points and review cycles.

## Research Limitations

- People analytics evidence varies across industries and country contexts.
- Some practitioner sources are useful but not peer-reviewed.
- AI-assisted analysis methods are changing rapidly.
- Final course materials should be reviewed before each intake for current AI tool and POPIA guidance.
