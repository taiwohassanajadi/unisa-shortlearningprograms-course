# Case Study and Worksheet Pack

## Case Study 1: Rising Turnover in a Support Unit

### Scenario

A university support unit has noticed that voluntary turnover increased over the past 12 months. Managers believe the problem is salary-related, but exit interview notes mention workload, unclear career paths and inconsistent supervision.

### Learner Task

Prepare a people analytics decision brief that:

- Frames a decision question.
- Identifies useful data sources.
- Defines metrics.
- Interprets the evidence cautiously.
- Recommends action.
- Includes an evaluation plan.

### Discussion Questions

- What data would be needed before blaming salary?
- What could exit interviews miss?
- What fairness or confidentiality risks exist?
- What intervention would be reasonable to test first?

## Case Study 2: Onboarding and Time to Productivity

### Scenario

New employees in different departments report very different onboarding experiences. Some become productive within one month, while others take three months or longer. HR wants to understand what is driving the difference.

### Learner Task

Create an analytics framing memo and data quality review.

### Discussion Questions

- How should "time to productivity" be defined?
- What data could be misleading?
- How can qualitative feedback be summarised responsibly?
- What action could improve onboarding?

## Case Study 3: Learning Programme Impact

### Scenario

An organisation runs a leadership development programme. Attendance is high, but senior leaders want to know whether the programme improves management behaviour.

### Learner Task

Design a decision-support approach that distinguishes attendance, satisfaction, learning and behaviour change.

### Discussion Questions

- Which metrics are easy but weak?
- What evidence would better show behaviour change?
- How should AI assist in summarising feedback?
- What are the limits of the available data?
