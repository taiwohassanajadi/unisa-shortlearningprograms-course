# Facilitator Guide

## Facilitator Role

The facilitator should help learners become better evidence users, not merely dashboard users. The emphasis is on decision quality, data quality, responsible AI-assisted interpretation and practical action.

## Teaching Principles

- Start with the decision, not the data.
- Keep analytics non-technical and manager-friendly.
- Use fictional or anonymised data.
- Emphasise POPIA, confidentiality and employee trust.
- Encourage learners to state uncertainty.
- Challenge unsupported causal claims.
- Treat AI outputs as drafts requiring human review.

## Suggested Weekly Facilitation Notes

### Week 1

Ask learners where HR or workforce decisions currently rely on intuition, incomplete reports or dashboards. Introduce the phrase: "analytics is useful only if it improves a decision."

### Week 2

Use examples of weak and strong metrics. Show that a metric can be easy to measure but still poor evidence for the decision.

### Week 3

Help learners refine decision questions. Push vague topics like "turnover" into decision-ready questions such as "Why is voluntary turnover higher among first-year employees in Department A?"

### Week 4

Teach data quality practically. Use a simple table with missing values, inconsistent categories and unclear definitions.

### Week 5

Focus on interpretation. Use misleading chart examples and ask learners what the chart proves, what it suggests and what it cannot answer.

### Week 6

Discuss POPIA and employee trust. Emphasise that people data should be used for specific, legitimate and proportionate purposes.

### Week 7

Demonstrate AI-assisted analysis using fictional data. Ask learners to critique the AI output for unsupported claims, missing context and overconfidence.

### Week 8

Teach analytics storytelling. A good decision brief should explain the question, evidence, limits and recommended action clearly.

### Week 9

Introduce analytics governance. Ask who owns the data, who approves analysis, who reviews fairness and who is accountable for decisions.

### Week 10

Move from insight to intervention. Make learners specify what should change, who will act and what resources are needed.

### Week 11

Focus on evaluation. Encourage balanced measures: outcome metrics, process metrics, employee experience, fairness and unintended consequences.

### Week 12

Final presentations should be decision-focused. Learners should explain what they recommend, what they are uncertain about and what should be monitored.

## Feedback Guidance

When giving feedback, focus on:

- Is the decision question clear?
- Are metrics properly defined?
- Are data limitations visible?
- Does the learner avoid causal overclaiming?
- Are POPIA and fairness risks addressed?
- Is AI-assisted interpretation checked?
- Is the recommendation realistic and measurable?

## Common Learner Challenges

| Challenge | Facilitator Response |
|---|---|
| Learner starts with a dashboard instead of a decision | Ask what decision the dashboard is meant to improve |
| Learner treats correlation as causation | Ask what alternative explanations exist |
| Learner uses vague metrics | Require definitions, numerator, denominator and time period |
| Learner trusts AI interpretation too quickly | Ask for evidence, source checks and uncertainty notes |
| Learner proposes action without evaluation | Require a measurement and review plan |

## Suggested Discussion Prompts

- What is a people metric that is commonly misused?
- When can analytics harm employee trust?
- What should managers disclose about people analytics?
- What decisions should never be automated?
- What makes an analytics recommendation credible?
