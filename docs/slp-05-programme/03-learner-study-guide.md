# Learner Study Guide

## Welcome

Welcome to **AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation**. This programme is for learners who want to connect AI-enabled work with sustainability, Green HRM, green skills and responsible digital transformation.

The course does not require coding or environmental engineering expertise. It focuses on how HRM and managers can help organisations adopt AI and digital work practices in ways that support sustainable performance, employee involvement and responsible resource use.

## How to Use This Study Guide

Each week you should:

1. Read the weekly study notes.
2. Complete the practical sustainability activity.
3. Add evidence to your final transformation plan.
4. Record risks, trade-offs and assumptions.
5. Participate in discussion or peer review.

## Core Principle

AI adoption and sustainability should not be managed as separate projects. Sustainable work transformation requires people systems, skills, employee involvement and responsible digital choices.

## Study Unit 1: Green HRM and Sustainable Work

### Key Ideas

Green HRM refers to HRM practices that support environmental management and sustainable organisational behaviour. It includes recruitment, learning, performance management, rewards, employee involvement, leadership development and workplace culture.

HRM can support sustainability by:

- Building green skills.
- Encouraging sustainable behaviour.
- Including sustainability in job roles.
- Training employees on responsible digital practices.
- Supporting employee voice and participation.
- Aligning performance and recognition with sustainable outcomes.

### Practical Focus

You will audit current Green HRM practices in a selected organisation, department or case.

### Reflection Question

Which HRM practices currently support sustainability, and which ones are silent on it?

## Study Unit 2: AI, Digital Work and Environmental Impact

### Key Ideas

AI and digital work have sustainability implications. Some AI tools can support sustainability by improving planning, reducing waste, enabling remote collaboration or identifying inefficiencies. At the same time, AI systems consume energy, rely on digital infrastructure and may contribute to wider environmental impacts across hardware, cloud, data and deployment lifecycles.

Green AI focuses on reducing the environmental footprint of AI systems. Sustainable AI is broader and considers social, ethical and environmental consequences across the AI lifecycle.

### Practical Focus

You will review one AI or digital work use case through a sustainability lens.

### Reflection Question

Is the AI or digital tool necessary, proportionate and aligned with the organisation's sustainability goals?

## Study Unit 3: Redesigning Work for Sustainable Performance

### Key Ideas

Sustainable work design considers productivity, wellbeing, inclusion and environmental impact together. AI-enabled work redesign should not only ask "Can this be faster?" It should also ask:

- Does this reduce waste?
- Does this improve employee wellbeing?
- Does this support inclusion?
- Does this build green skills?
- Does this avoid unnecessary digital activity?
- Does this improve service quality?

### Practical Focus

You will design a green skills and employee involvement plan for a selected AI-enabled work process.

### Reflection Question

How can employees participate in shaping sustainable AI-enabled work rather than merely receiving instructions?

## Study Unit 4: Implementation, Metrics and ESG-Aligned Reporting

### Key Ideas

Sustainability work needs measurement, but the metrics should be useful and honest. Sustainable HRM metrics may include green skills participation, employee involvement, reduced process waste, sustainable behaviour adoption, digital tool use, employee wellbeing indicators and ESG-related evidence.

The purpose of reporting is not only to produce numbers. It is to support accountability, learning and better decisions.

### Practical Focus

You will build a practical implementation roadmap and metrics plan.

### Reflection Question

What evidence would show that the transformation improved sustainability and work quality?

## Final Portfolio

Your final portfolio is a **Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan**. It should help an organisation or department connect AI-enabled work redesign with Green HRM, green skills, employee involvement and ESG-aligned evidence.
