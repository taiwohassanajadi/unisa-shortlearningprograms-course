# Facilitator Guide

## Facilitator Role

The facilitator should help learners connect sustainability, HRM and AI-enabled work in practical ways. The course should avoid vague sustainability language and instead push learners toward specific HRM practices, skills, behaviours, metrics and implementation plans.

## Teaching Principles

- Keep the course practical and manager-friendly.
- Treat sustainability as a people and work-design issue.
- Distinguish Green AI from Sustainable AI.
- Discuss trade-offs honestly.
- Use workplace scenarios rather than abstract environmental claims.
- Encourage employee involvement and change participation.
- Avoid overstating what HRM alone can control.

## Suggested Weekly Facilitation Notes

### Week 1

Help learners see that AI adoption and sustainability are connected. Ask where digital work creates value, waste, inclusion benefits or environmental costs.

### Week 2

Introduce Green HRM through familiar HR practices: recruitment, learning, performance, rewards and employee involvement.

### Week 3

Focus on green skills. Learners should identify practical skills needed for sustainable work, not only climate awareness.

### Week 4

Discuss AI's environmental and social footprint. Keep this balanced: AI may support sustainability but also has resource implications.

### Week 5

Teach Green AI and Sustainable AI distinctions. Green AI is environmental-efficiency focused; Sustainable AI is broader and includes social, ethical and governance impacts.

### Week 6

Use hybrid and digital work trade-offs. Remote work may reduce commuting but increase digital workload, isolation or infrastructure demands.

### Week 7

Redesign work using four lenses: productivity, wellbeing, inclusion and sustainability.

### Week 8

Focus on employee involvement. Sustainable change is more credible when employees can contribute ideas and feedback.

### Week 9

Teach metrics carefully. Not every sustainability outcome is easy to measure. Encourage proxy metrics but require limitations.

### Week 10

Connect HRM work to ESG-aligned evidence. The aim is practical reporting, not inflated claims.

### Week 11

Build implementation roadmaps. Ask learners to define owners, timelines, resources, risks and review cycles.

### Week 12

Final presentations should explain the transformation plan, why it is practical and how it will be monitored.

## Feedback Guidance

When giving feedback, focus on:

- Is the sustainability problem specific?
- Are HRM practices clearly connected to sustainability?
- Is AI use necessary and proportionate?
- Are employee involvement mechanisms meaningful?
- Are green skills practical?
- Are metrics realistic?
- Is the roadmap implementable?

## Common Learner Challenges

| Challenge | Facilitator Response |
|---|---|
| Learner uses vague sustainability claims | Ask for specific practice, evidence and metric |
| Learner treats AI as automatically sustainable | Ask about energy, infrastructure, procurement and necessity |
| Learner ignores employees | Require employee involvement and feedback mechanisms |
| Learner focuses only on ESG reporting | Redirect to workplace practice and capability-building |
| Learner proposes too broad a transformation | Narrow to one department or process |

## Suggested Discussion Prompts

- What HRM practice has the strongest influence on sustainable behaviour?
- When does AI use become unnecessary digital waste?
- What green skills do managers need in AI-enabled workplaces?
- How can employees shape sustainable digital work?
- What should be reported, and what should simply be improved?
