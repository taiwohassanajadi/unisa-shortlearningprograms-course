# Programme Specification

## Programme Title

**AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation**

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Academic Home

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HRM practitioners, departmental heads, sustainability managers, ESG teams, organisational development practitioners, learning and development teams, public sector managers, internal auditors, consultants and postgraduate learners interested in sustainable digital work.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have:

- Basic digital literacy.
- Interest or experience in HRM, management, sustainability, ESG, organisational development, learning and development, auditing or public sector transformation.
- Ability to analyse workplace practices and propose practical interventions.
- No coding, data science or environmental engineering requirement.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to design HRM practices that support sustainable work transformation in AI-enabled organisations, with attention to Green HRM, green skills, employee involvement, sustainable AI use and ESG-aligned reporting.

## Problem Statement

AI adoption is often treated as a productivity issue, while sustainability is treated as a separate ESG issue. In practice, organisations need people systems that support both: green skills, sustainable work practices, responsible digital adoption and awareness of AI's environmental footprint.

This SLP connects Green HRM with AI-enabled work transformation. It helps learners evaluate AI-enabled work through productivity, inclusion and sustainability lenses.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

On successful completion, learners should be able to:

1. Explain Green HRM and its relevance to AI-enabled workplaces.
2. Identify sustainability implications of AI adoption and digital work.
3. Design HR practices that support green skills, employee involvement and sustainable work behaviour.
4. Evaluate AI-enabled work redesign through productivity, inclusion and sustainability lenses.
5. Produce a Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan for a selected organisation, department or case.

## Programme Structure

The programme has four study units and one capstone portfolio:

- **AHRM05A:** Green HRM and Sustainable Work
- **AHRM05B:** AI, Digital Work and Environmental Impact
- **AHRM05C:** Redesigning Work for Sustainable Performance
- **AHRM05D:** Implementation, Metrics and ESG-Aligned Reporting
- **Capstone:** Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan

## Assessment Structure

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

## Capstone Output

Learners produce a **Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan** containing:

- Organisational or departmental context.
- Current Green HRM practice audit.
- Selected AI-enabled work process.
- Sustainability risks and opportunities.
- Green skills plan.
- Employee involvement strategy.
- Sustainable AI procurement or use checklist.
- Metrics and ESG-aligned reporting plan.
- Implementation roadmap.

## Graduate Attributes

The programme supports:

- Sustainability-oriented leadership.
- Responsible digital transformation.
- Evidence-informed HRM practice.
- Employee involvement and change capability.
- ESG and reporting awareness.
- Practical green skills development.
