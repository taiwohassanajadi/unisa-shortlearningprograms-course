# Reading List and Research Notes

## Review Scope

This reading list supports SLP 05: **AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation**. It uses a rapid scoping approach focused on:

- Green HRM and sustainable HRM.
- Green skills and employee involvement.
- AI, digital work and sustainability.
- Green AI and Sustainable AI.
- ESG-aligned HRM metrics and reporting.
- Responsible AI procurement and use.

## Evidence Synthesis

### Green HRM Makes Sustainability a People-System Issue

Green HRM literature shows that sustainability depends on employee capability, motivation and involvement. HRM practices such as recruitment, learning, performance, rewards and employee voice can support sustainable workplace behaviour.

### AI Adoption Has Sustainability Trade-Offs

AI can support sustainability through better planning, waste reduction, remote collaboration and process optimisation. It can also create environmental burdens through computing infrastructure, energy use, water use, hardware and increased digital activity. The course therefore teaches learners to ask whether AI use is necessary, proportionate and aligned with sustainability goals.

### Green AI and Sustainable AI Are Related but Not Identical

Green AI focuses mainly on reducing environmental impact. Sustainable AI is broader and includes environmental, social, ethical and governance dimensions. HRM can support both through skills, procurement questions, employee involvement and responsible work redesign.

### Green Skills Are a Workforce Priority

Workforce transformation increasingly requires both digital and sustainability skills. The SLP positions HRM as a key function for developing green skills in AI-enabled workplaces.

### Audit and ESG Lenses Strengthen the Programme

The Department of Auditing lens adds value through evidence, measurement, reporting discipline and accountability. Learners should avoid unsupported sustainability claims and develop metrics that can support ESG-aligned reporting and improvement.

## Research Limitations

- Green AI research is developing quickly and measurement methods remain uneven.
- Environmental impacts vary by AI model, infrastructure, location, use pattern and lifecycle boundary.
- Green HRM evidence may vary across sectors and countries.
- Final course materials should be reviewed before each intake for current AI sustainability and ESG reporting guidance.
