# Curriculum and Weekly Learning Matrix

## Weekly Structure

The course is designed for 12 weeks. Each week includes guided study, applied sustainability practice, discussion and contribution to the final transformation plan.

| Week | Study Unit | Topic | Learning Outcomes | Learning Activities | Assessment Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AHRM05A | Orientation: AI, Green HRM and sustainable work | Explain the course purpose and identify links between AI, HRM and sustainability | Sustainability self-audit, workplace discussion | Portfolio context note |
| 2 | AHRM05A | Green HRM foundations | Explain Green HRM practices and employee involvement | Green HRM practice mapping | Short reflection |
| 3 | AHRM05A | Green skills and sustainability capability | Identify green skills needs and HRM levers | Green skills gap activity | Formative task 1 submission |
| 4 | AHRM05B | AI, digital work and environmental impact | Explain sustainability implications of AI and digital work | AI footprint discussion, tool-use mapping | Formative task 2 preparation |
| 5 | AHRM05B | Green AI, Sustainable AI and responsible procurement | Distinguish Green AI and Sustainable AI and ask procurement questions | Procurement checklist, lifecycle thinking exercise | Sustainable AI review draft |
| 6 | AHRM05B | Remote, hybrid and digital collaboration trade-offs | Evaluate sustainability trade-offs in digital work | Hybrid work scenario analysis | Formative task 2 submission |
| 7 | AHRM05C | Work redesign for sustainable performance | Redesign work for productivity, wellbeing and sustainability | Work redesign workshop | Formative task 3 preparation |
| 8 | AHRM05C | Employee involvement and sustainable behaviour change | Design employee involvement and green behaviour interventions | Employee involvement plan | Formative task 3 submission |
| 9 | AHRM05D | Sustainable HRM metrics | Define sustainable HRM and green skills metrics | Metrics clinic | Portfolio draft |
| 10 | AHRM05D | ESG and HR reporting alignment | Link HRM actions to ESG and reporting needs | Reporting map, evidence checklist | Portfolio draft |
| 11 | AHRM05D | Implementation roadmap and review cycle | Build a practical roadmap and governance cycle | Roadmap workshop, peer review | Portfolio draft review |
| 12 | Capstone | Final transformation plan presentation | Present a defensible Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan | Peer presentation, final reflection | Final portfolio submission |

## Study Unit Outcomes

### AHRM05A - Green HRM and Sustainable Work

Learners should be able to:

- Explain Green HRM and sustainable HRM.
- Identify HRM practices that support environmental and sustainability goals.
- Explain green skills and sustainability capability.
- Analyse employee involvement in sustainable workplace change.

### AHRM05B - AI, Digital Work and Environmental Impact

Learners should be able to:

- Identify sustainability implications of AI adoption.
- Distinguish Green AI and Sustainable AI.
- Assess environmental and social trade-offs in digital work.
- Develop responsible AI procurement and use questions.

### AHRM05C - Redesigning Work for Sustainable Performance

Learners should be able to:

- Redesign work through productivity, wellbeing, inclusion and sustainability lenses.
- Link AI-supported work redesign to green skills and employee development.
- Reduce waste in HR and workplace processes.
- Design employee involvement strategies.

### AHRM05D - Implementation, Metrics and ESG-Aligned Reporting

Learners should be able to:

- Define sustainable HRM metrics.
- Align HRM actions with ESG and reporting requirements.
- Build an implementation roadmap.
- Design a review and improvement cycle.

## Workload Estimate

Recommended weekly workload:

- Guided reading: 2 hours.
- Learning material and recorded lecture: 1 hour.
- Practical sustainability activity: 2 hours.
- Discussion or peer review: 1 hour.
- Portfolio development: 1 to 2 hours.

Estimated total workload: 84 to 96 hours.
