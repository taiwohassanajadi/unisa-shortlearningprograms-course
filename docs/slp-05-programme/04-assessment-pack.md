# Assessment Pack and Rubrics

## Assessment Overview

The course uses an applied sustainability transformation portfolio model. Each formative task builds toward the final Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan.

| Assessment | Weight | Due | Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Formative Task 1 | 10% | Week 3 | Green HRM practice audit |
| Formative Task 2 | 15% | Week 6 | Sustainable AI use-case review |
| Formative Task 3 | 15% | Week 8 | Green skills and employee involvement plan |
| Final Portfolio | 60% | Week 12 | Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan |

## Formative Task 1: Green HRM Practice Audit

### Purpose

Learners evaluate current HRM practices through a sustainability lens.

### Instructions

Select one organisation, department or case. Submit a 1,000 to 1,200 word Green HRM audit covering:

- Organisational or departmental context.
- Current sustainability priorities.
- HRM practices reviewed.
- Green recruitment, learning, performance, rewards and employee involvement practices.
- Current gaps.
- Priority improvement areas.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Context and sustainability framing | 20% |
| Green HRM practice analysis | 30% |
| Gap identification | 25% |
| Practical priorities | 15% |
| Clarity and professionalism | 10% |

## Formative Task 2: Sustainable AI Use-Case Review

### Purpose

Learners evaluate one AI or digital work use case through sustainability, inclusion and responsible use lenses.

### Instructions

Select one AI-enabled or digital work process. Submit a review covering:

- Use-case description.
- Intended benefits.
- Environmental and digital infrastructure considerations.
- Social and employee-experience implications.
- Responsible procurement or tool-use questions.
- Green AI or Sustainable AI considerations.
- Recommendation: proceed, proceed with controls, redesign, or avoid.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Use-case clarity | 15% |
| Sustainability analysis | 25% |
| Employee and inclusion implications | 20% |
| Responsible AI and procurement questions | 20% |
| Practical recommendation | 20% |

## Formative Task 3: Green Skills and Employee Involvement Plan

### Purpose

Learners design a practical plan to build green skills and involve employees in sustainable AI-enabled work.

### Instructions

Submit a plan covering:

- Green skills needed.
- Target learner groups.
- Learning activities.
- Employee participation mechanisms.
- Change communication.
- Barriers and enablers.
- Metrics for participation and behaviour change.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Green skills analysis | 25% |
| Employee involvement design | 25% |
| Change and communication plan | 20% |
| Metrics and evaluation | 20% |
| Professional structure | 10% |

## Final Portfolio: Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan

### Purpose

Learners integrate the course learning into a practical transformation plan for one organisation, department or case.

### Required Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Organisational or departmental context.
3. Sustainability problem and AI-enabled work opportunity.
4. Current Green HRM practice audit.
5. Selected AI or digital work use-case review.
6. Sustainability risks and opportunities.
7. Green skills plan.
8. Employee involvement strategy.
9. Responsible AI and sustainable procurement checklist.
10. Metrics and ESG-aligned reporting plan.
11. Implementation roadmap.
12. Limitations and improvement cycle.

Recommended length: 4,000 to 5,500 words.

### Final Portfolio Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Context and sustainability problem framing | 10% |
| Green HRM practice audit | 15% |
| AI or digital work sustainability review | 15% |
| Green skills plan | 15% |
| Employee involvement and change strategy | 15% |
| Responsible AI and procurement considerations | 10% |
| Metrics, ESG alignment and implementation roadmap | 15% |
| Professional structure and communication | 5% |

## AI Use in Assessment

Learners may use AI tools for brainstorming, drafting or reviewing structure, but they must:

- Disclose how AI was used.
- Avoid uploading personal, confidential or sensitive organisational data.
- Verify AI-generated content.
- Consider whether the AI use itself is necessary and proportionate.
- Take responsibility for final recommendations.
