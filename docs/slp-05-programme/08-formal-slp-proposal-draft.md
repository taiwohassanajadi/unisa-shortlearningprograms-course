# Formal SLP Proposal Draft

## Programme Title

**AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation**

## Host College, Department and Centres

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Motivation

AI adoption and sustainability are often treated as separate organisational agendas. In practice, they increasingly intersect. AI and digital work can improve productivity, reduce some forms of waste and support new ways of working, but they can also create environmental and social trade-offs. HRM is central to this transformation because sustainable work depends on skills, employee involvement, leadership behaviour, culture and measurement.

This SLP gives learners a practical method for connecting Green HRM, responsible AI use and sustainable work transformation.

## Target Group

The programme is aimed at:

- HRM practitioners.
- Departmental heads.
- Sustainability managers.
- ESG teams.
- Organisational development practitioners.
- Learning and development teams.
- Public sector managers.
- Internal auditors.
- Consultants.
- Postgraduate learners and researchers.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to design HRM practices that support sustainable work transformation in AI-enabled organisations, with attention to Green HRM, green skills, employee involvement, sustainable AI use and ESG-aligned reporting.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

Learners who complete the programme should be able to:

1. Explain Green HRM and its relevance to AI-enabled workplaces.
2. Identify sustainability implications of AI adoption and digital work.
3. Design HR practices that support green skills, employee involvement and sustainable work behaviour.
4. Evaluate AI-enabled work redesign through productivity, inclusion and sustainability lenses.
5. Produce a Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan for a selected organisation, department or case.

## Programme Duration

12 weeks.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Language Medium

English.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have basic digital literacy and an interest in HRM, management, sustainability, ESG, organisational development, learning and development, auditing or public sector transformation. Coding, data science or environmental engineering knowledge is not required.

## Programme Content

### Module 1: Green HRM and Sustainable Work

Purpose: To introduce Green HRM and show how HRM practices support sustainable workplace behaviour.

Areas covered:

- Green HRM foundations.
- HRM practices for sustainability.
- Green skills.
- Employee involvement.
- Sustainable culture and behaviour.

### Module 2: AI, Digital Work and Environmental Impact

Purpose: To help learners evaluate sustainability implications of AI adoption and digital work.

Areas covered:

- AI and digital work transformation.
- Environmental footprint of AI systems.
- Green AI and Sustainable AI.
- Responsible AI procurement.
- Hybrid work and digital collaboration trade-offs.

### Module 3: Redesigning Work for Sustainable Performance

Purpose: To teach learners how to redesign work through productivity, wellbeing, inclusion and sustainability lenses.

Areas covered:

- Sustainable work redesign.
- AI-supported learning and skills development.
- Reducing process waste.
- Sustainable employee experience.
- Inclusion and access in digital work.
- Change management for sustainable behaviour.

### Module 4: Implementation, Metrics and ESG-Aligned Reporting

Purpose: To enable learners to implement, measure and report sustainable AI + HRM transformation responsibly.

Areas covered:

- Sustainable HRM metrics.
- Green skills indicators.
- Employee involvement evidence.
- AI use metrics.
- ESG and HR reporting alignment.
- Implementation roadmap and review cycle.

## Assessment

The programme uses a portfolio-based assessment model:

- Formative task 1: Green HRM practice audit, 10%.
- Formative task 2: Sustainable AI use-case review, 15%.
- Formative task 3: Green skills and employee involvement plan, 15%.
- Final portfolio: Sustainable AI + HRM Transformation Plan, 60%.

## Final Portfolio

The final portfolio requires learners to prepare a transformation plan for one organisation, department or case. It includes:

- Organisational context.
- Green HRM practice audit.
- AI-enabled work process review.
- Sustainability risks and opportunities.
- Green skills plan.
- Employee involvement strategy.
- Responsible AI and procurement checklist.
- Metrics and ESG-aligned reporting plan.
- Implementation roadmap.
