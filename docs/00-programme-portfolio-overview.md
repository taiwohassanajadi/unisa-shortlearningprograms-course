# Programme Portfolio Overview

## Working Title

**AI + HRM Short Learning Programme Portfolio for Managers, Practitioners and Applied Researchers**

## Purpose

This portfolio proposes five standalone Short Learning Programme (SLP) concepts for the University of South Africa context. The portfolio is designed around one core problem: departmental heads, team leads, managers and HR professionals are being asked to use AI in workplace decisions before they have reliable frameworks for work redesign, governance, analytics, automation, and sustainable implementation.

The five SLPs are deliberately practical. Each concept can stand alone as a short course, but together they form a coherent portfolio on AI-enabled human resource management.

## UNISA-Style Positioning

The UNISA example reviewed for this portfolio uses a clear public-facing format: qualification code, duration, language medium, target group, registration details, course leader, programme administrator, and modules. It also frames each module by purpose and content areas rather than by lengthy academic descriptions. This portfolio follows that style so that the concepts can later be converted into formal UNISA SLP proposal documents.

Recommended default format for all five concepts:

- **Duration:** 12 weeks
- **Language medium:** English
- **Delivery mode:** Online or blended distance learning
- **Level:** Applied undergraduate/postgraduate bridge, suitable for managers and postgraduate learners
- **Assessment model:** 40% formative tasks, 60% final applied portfolio
- **Credential logic:** Non-degree SLP / certificate of competence, subject to UNISA approval
- **Academic home:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS), Department of Auditing.
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa, in collaboration with the Centre for Lifelong Learning.

## The Five SLP Concepts

1. **AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**
   - Focus: practical use of ChatGPT, Claude and similar LLMs for workplace and people-management tasks, with emphasis on redesigning work rather than only prompting tools.
   - Best audience: departmental heads, team leads, line managers, HR business partners, HRD practitioners, operations leads, project leads and postgraduate learners in management.

2. **Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance**
   - Focus: fairness, accountability, privacy, transparency, human oversight, POPIA, international AI governance standards, and high-risk HR use cases.
   - Best audience: HR leaders, compliance officers, public sector managers, consultants and researchers.

3. **People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers**
   - Focus: using HR data, dashboards and AI-assisted analysis responsibly for workforce decisions.
   - Best audience: HR managers, analysts, OD practitioners, talent managers and postgraduate learners.

4. **Agentic HRM and Workflow Automation for Managers**
   - Focus: AI agents, workflow redesign, automation readiness, human-in-the-loop controls and HR operating models.
   - Best audience: HR operations teams, service delivery managers, transformation leads and applied AI consultants.

5. **AI, Green HRM and Sustainable Work Transformation**
   - Focus: linking AI adoption with sustainability, Green HRM, green skills, digital work practices and environmentally responsible AI.
   - Best audience: HRM practitioners, sustainability managers, ESG teams, organisational development specialists and researchers.

## Shared Learning Design

Each SLP should contain four study units plus a capstone:

- **Study Unit 1:** Concepts, context and managerial relevance
- **Study Unit 2:** Tools, methods and applied practice
- **Study Unit 3:** Risk, ethics, law and governance
- **Study Unit 4:** Implementation, evaluation and change management
- **Capstone:** A workplace-ready artefact such as an AI use policy, work redesign map, analytics dashboard brief, agent workflow blueprint, or sustainable AI-HRM implementation plan

## Portfolio Rationale

The evidence base points to three recurring themes:

- AI is reshaping job tasks and skills, but the impact depends on how organisations redesign work, govern systems and support learning.
- HR use cases are high-stakes because they affect hiring, development, performance, access to opportunity, employee privacy and workplace power.
- Managers need practical capability, not only technical awareness: they must decide where AI should assist, where humans must remain accountable, and how outcomes will be measured.
