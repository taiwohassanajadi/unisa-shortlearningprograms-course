# Course Development Roadmap

## Recommended Development Approach

Develop the portfolio in two waves:

- **Wave 1:** Build the flagship course, **AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**.
- **Wave 2:** Build the remaining four courses using the same template, shared readings and shared assessment architecture.

This reduces development risk because the flagship course can validate learner demand, tone, assessment difficulty and institutional approval expectations before the full portfolio is produced.

## Phase 1 - Concept Approval Pack

Deliverables:

- One-page rationale for the full portfolio.
- Five UNISA-style concept notes.
- Proposed target groups and learning outcomes.
- Initial evidence matrix.
- Recommended academic home and collaboration units.
- Draft admission assumptions.

Quality checks:

- Each concept solves a clear workplace problem.
- Each concept can stand alone.
- Each concept has four coherent study units and one capstone artefact.
- No concept depends on learners having advanced coding skills.

## Phase 2 - Curriculum Matrix

Create a matrix for each SLP covering:

- Exit-level outcomes.
- Study unit outcomes.
- Weekly topics.
- Required and recommended readings.
- Learning activities.
- Formative assessments.
- Final portfolio requirements.
- Marking rubric.

Default weekly rhythm:

- Week 1: Orientation and core concepts.
- Weeks 2-3: Evidence and frameworks.
- Weeks 4-6: Applied methods and tool practice.
- Weeks 7-9: Risk, governance and implementation.
- Weeks 10-11: Capstone development.
- Week 12: Presentation, reflection and final submission.

## Phase 3 - Learning Material Development

For each study unit, develop:

- Study guide chapter.
- Slide deck.
- Short video script.
- Practical worksheet.
- Case scenario.
- Reading list.
- Knowledge check.
- Assessment instructions.

Recommended case style:

- Use South African workplace scenarios where possible.
- Include public sector, private sector and SME examples.
- Avoid asking learners to upload sensitive employee data into AI tools.
- Use fictional or anonymised datasets for analytics tasks.

## Phase 4 - Assessment Design

Use a portfolio model because these courses are applied and professional.

Recommended weighting:

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

Common rubric dimensions:

- Problem framing.
- Evidence use.
- Practicality of proposed solution.
- Responsible AI and POPIA awareness.
- Human oversight and governance.
- Measurement and evaluation.
- Clarity of professional communication.

## Phase 5 - Review and Validation

Before launch, validate with:

- HRM academic reviewer.
- AI / information systems reviewer.
- Legal or POPIA-informed reviewer.
- Practitioner reviewer from HR or organisational development.
- Learning design reviewer.

Validation questions:

- Is the course academically credible?
- Is it accessible to non-technical managers?
- Are legal and ethical risks handled responsibly?
- Are assessments realistic for working adults?
- Does the course fit UNISA SLP expectations?

## Phase 6 - Pilot and Improvement

Pilot the flagship course with a small group, then refine:

- Workload.
- Clarity of activities.
- Rubric reliability.
- Learner support needs.
- AI tool access assumptions.
- Case relevance.
- Completion rates and learner feedback.
