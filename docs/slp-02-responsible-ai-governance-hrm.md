# SLP 02: Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HR leaders, HR compliance practitioners, public sector managers, data protection officers, labour relations practitioners, consultants, postgraduate learners and researchers concerned with responsible AI use in employment contexts.

## Problem Being Solved

HRM is a high-stakes domain for AI because it shapes recruitment, screening, promotion, performance management, training, employee monitoring and workplace opportunity. Poorly governed AI can amplify discrimination, violate privacy, weaken transparency and damage trust.

This SLP gives managers and practitioners a governance toolkit for deciding whether an HR AI use case should proceed, what controls are needed, and how to document accountability.

## Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to identify, assess and govern ethical, legal and operational risks associated with AI-enabled HRM systems.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion, learners should be able to:

- Explain key responsible AI principles in HRM.
- Identify high-risk HR AI use cases and affected stakeholders.
- Apply POPIA, responsible AI and AI risk management concepts to HR scenarios.
- Create an AI risk register and impact assessment for an HRM use case.
- Recommend governance controls, human oversight and monitoring practices.

## Study Units

### AHRM02A - Responsible AI Principles for HRM

Content areas:

- Fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, robustness and human oversight.
- Why HRM differs from low-stakes AI use.
- Employee rights, power imbalance and procedural fairness.
- AI harms in recruitment, development, performance and monitoring.
- International principles and standards.

### AHRM02B - Legal and Regulatory Context

Content areas:

- POPIA and employee personal information.
- Automated decision-making and data subject rights.
- Special personal information and HR data sensitivity.
- International reference points: NIST AI RMF, OECD AI Principles, ISO/IEC 42001 and the EU AI Act risk-based approach.
- Vendor accountability, procurement and third-party AI tools.

### AHRM02C - Bias, Discrimination and Fairness Auditing

Content areas:

- Algorithmic discrimination in hiring and HR development.
- Data quality, representativeness and proxy variables.
- Fairness testing and documentation.
- Explainability and contestability.
- Human review, appeal and grievance processes.

### AHRM02D - AI Governance Operating Model for HR

Content areas:

- HR AI policy and acceptable use.
- Roles and responsibilities: HR, IT, legal, procurement, line managers and workers.
- AI risk register.
- AI impact assessment.
- Monitoring, audit and incident response.
- Governance communication and training.

## Assessment

- **Formative task 1:** Stakeholder and risk map for an HR AI use case.
- **Formative task 2:** POPIA and responsible AI compliance checklist.
- **Formative task 3:** Fairness and bias review memo.
- **Final portfolio:** HR AI governance pack for a selected use case.

## Capstone Artefact

Learners produce a **Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM** containing:

- Use-case description.
- Stakeholder map.
- Risk register.
- AI impact assessment.
- Human oversight design.
- Data protection considerations.
- Monitoring and incident-response plan.

## Evidence Base

Research on AI in HRM highlights accountability, fairness, legal and employee reaction risks. Official AI governance frameworks provide useful structures, but these must be translated into practical HR routines: procurement checks, policy, risk assessment, human review, documentation and worker communication.
