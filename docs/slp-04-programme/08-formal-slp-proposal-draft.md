# Formal SLP Proposal Draft

## Programme Title

**Agentic HRM and Workflow Automation for Managers**

## Host College, Department and Centres

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Motivation

Organisations are beginning to move from AI chatbots and copilots toward AI agents that can plan steps, retrieve information, call tools and trigger actions across systems. In HRM, this creates opportunities to improve employee service delivery, onboarding, recruitment coordination, learning administration and HR case routing. It also introduces new risks around autonomy, permissions, data exposure, accountability, employee trust and audit evidence.

This SLP gives managers and practitioners a practical method for evaluating whether an HR workflow is ready for agentic automation, how human oversight should be designed and how a controlled pilot should be governed.

## Target Group

The programme is aimed at:

- HR operations managers.
- Departmental heads.
- Service delivery managers.
- HR transformation teams.
- Shared-services leaders.
- Internal auditors.
- Risk and compliance practitioners.
- Consultants.
- Line managers.
- Postgraduate learners and researchers.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to evaluate, design and govern AI-agent and workflow-automation use cases in HRM, with emphasis on readiness, permissions, human oversight, auditability, risk control and measurable pilots.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

Learners who complete the programme should be able to:

1. Explain the difference between chatbots, copilots, workflow automation and AI agents.
2. Identify HRM workflows that may benefit from agentic automation or AI-assisted orchestration.
3. Map process steps, data access, permissions, handoffs, failure points and escalation paths.
4. Design human-in-the-loop controls, audit trails and governance boundaries for HR AI agents.
5. Build a low-risk pilot proposal for an agentic HRM workflow.

## Programme Duration

12 weeks.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Language Medium

English.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have basic digital literacy and an interest in HRM, operations, service delivery, auditing, risk, compliance, transformation or process improvement. Coding knowledge is not required.

## Programme Content

### Module 1: From Chatbots to AI Agents in HRM

Purpose: To introduce agentic AI and clarify how it differs from chatbots, copilots and rule-based automation.

Areas covered:

- Chatbots, copilots, workflow automation and AI agents.
- HRM use cases.
- Automation versus augmentation.
- Human agency.
- Current maturity and hype risks.

### Module 2: HR Workflow Mapping and Automation Readiness

Purpose: To teach learners how to assess whether a workflow is ready for agentic automation.

Areas covered:

- Process discovery.
- Workflow mapping.
- Task suitability.
- Data access and system integration.
- Failure points and exceptions.
- Readiness criteria.

### Module 3: Human-in-the-Loop Agent Workflow Design

Purpose: To enable learners to design agent-assisted workflows with human review, escalation and accountability.

Areas covered:

- Agent roles and boundaries.
- Permission mapping.
- Human approval gates.
- Escalation rules.
- Logs and audit trails.
- Prohibited actions.

### Module 4: Pilot, Evaluation, Governance and Scaling

Purpose: To teach learners how to pilot, evaluate, scale or retire agentic HRM workflows responsibly.

Areas covered:

- Low-risk pilot design.
- Vendor and security review.
- Success metrics.
- Risk incident monitoring.
- Scale, revise, pause or retire decisions.
- Continuous governance.

## Assessment

The programme uses a portfolio-based assessment model:

- Formative task 1: HR workflow automation readiness assessment, 10%.
- Formative task 2: Current workflow and exception map, 15%.
- Formative task 3: Agent risk and permission map, 15%.
- Final portfolio: Agentic HRM Workflow Blueprint, 60%.

## Final Portfolio

The final portfolio requires learners to prepare an agentic workflow blueprint for one HRM or people-management process. It includes:

- Current workflow.
- Automation readiness assessment.
- Proposed agent-assisted future workflow.
- Data and permission map.
- Human review points.
- Escalation rules.
- Risk register.
- Audit trail and monitoring plan.
- Pilot evaluation plan.
