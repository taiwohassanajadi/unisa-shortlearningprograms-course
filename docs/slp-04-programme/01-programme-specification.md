# Programme Specification

## Programme Title

**Agentic HRM and Workflow Automation for Managers**

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Academic Home

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HR operations managers, departmental heads, service delivery managers, HR transformation teams, shared-services leaders, internal auditors, risk and compliance practitioners, consultants, line managers and postgraduate learners interested in AI agents and workflow automation in HRM.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have:

- Basic digital literacy.
- Interest or experience in HRM, operations, service delivery, auditing, risk, compliance, transformation or process improvement.
- Ability to describe workplace processes and identify handoffs.
- No coding or AI engineering requirement.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to evaluate, design and govern AI-agent and workflow-automation use cases in HRM, with emphasis on readiness, permissions, human oversight, auditability, risk control and measurable pilots.

## Problem Statement

Organisations are moving from chatbots and copilots to AI agents that can plan steps, use tools, retrieve information, draft outputs and trigger actions across systems. In HRM this may improve service delivery, but it also creates risks: over-automation, unclear accountability, data exposure, bias, weak escalation paths, tool misuse and reduced human agency.

This SLP helps learners identify workflows suitable for agentic AI, design human-in-the-loop controls and build an automation readiness case before implementation.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

On successful completion, learners should be able to:

1. Explain the difference between chatbots, copilots, workflow automation and AI agents.
2. Identify HRM workflows that may benefit from agentic automation or AI-assisted orchestration.
3. Map process steps, data access, permissions, handoffs, failure points and escalation paths.
4. Design human-in-the-loop controls, audit trails and governance boundaries for HR AI agents.
5. Build a low-risk pilot proposal for an agentic HRM workflow.

## Programme Structure

The programme has four study units and one capstone portfolio:

- **AHRM04A:** From Chatbots to AI Agents in HRM
- **AHRM04B:** HR Workflow Mapping and Automation Readiness
- **AHRM04C:** Human-in-the-Loop Agent Workflow Design
- **AHRM04D:** Pilot, Evaluation, Governance and Scaling
- **Capstone:** Agentic HRM Workflow Blueprint

## Assessment Structure

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

## Capstone Output

Learners produce an **Agentic HRM Workflow Blueprint** containing:

- Selected HRM or people-management process.
- Current workflow map.
- Automation readiness assessment.
- Proposed agent-assisted workflow.
- Data, system and permission map.
- Human approval gates.
- Escalation rules.
- Risk register.
- Audit trail and monitoring plan.
- Pilot evaluation plan.

## Graduate Attributes

The programme supports:

- Responsible digital transformation capability.
- Audit-minded process control.
- Human-centred automation design.
- Risk-aware AI adoption.
- Practical workflow redesign.
- Evidence-informed pilot planning.
