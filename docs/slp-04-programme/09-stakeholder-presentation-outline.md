# Stakeholder Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

**Agentic HRM and Workflow Automation for Managers**

Subtitle: Proposed Short Learning Programme under CAS, Department of Auditing, Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, and Centre for Lifelong Learning.

## Slide 2: Why This Programme, Why Now

- AI is moving from chatbots to agents that can perform multi-step tasks.
- HRM workflows may benefit from faster triage, routing, retrieval and administration.
- Agentic systems introduce new risks around access, autonomy and accountability.
- Managers need readiness and governance skills before deployment.

## Slide 3: The Problem

Agentic AI can fail when:

- Workflows are unclear.
- Agents receive too much access.
- Human review is vague.
- Exceptions are not planned.
- Logs and audit trails are weak.
- Success is measured only by speed.

## Slide 4: Target Learners

- HR operations managers.
- Departmental heads.
- Service delivery managers.
- HR transformation teams.
- Shared-services leaders.
- Internal auditors.
- Risk and compliance practitioners.
- Consultants, line managers and postgraduate learners.

## Slide 5: Programme Promise

Learners will leave with a practical **Agentic HRM Workflow Blueprint** for a controlled, low-risk HRM pilot.

## Slide 6: Programme Structure

- Week 1 to 3: Agentic AI concepts and task suitability.
- Week 4 to 6: Workflow mapping and automation readiness.
- Week 7 to 8: Human oversight, permissions and audit trails.
- Week 9 to 11: Pilot, governance, evaluation and scaling.
- Week 12: Final workflow blueprint presentation.

## Slide 7: Four Study Units

1. From Chatbots to AI Agents in HRM.
2. HR Workflow Mapping and Automation Readiness.
3. Human-in-the-Loop Agent Workflow Design.
4. Pilot, Evaluation, Governance and Scaling.

## Slide 8: Assessment Model

- HR workflow automation readiness assessment: 10%.
- Current workflow and exception map: 15%.
- Agent risk and permission map: 15%.
- Final agentic workflow blueprint: 60%.

## Slide 9: Responsible Agentic AI

The programme integrates:

- Workflow mapping.
- Permission design.
- Human approval gates.
- Escalation paths.
- Audit trails.
- Risk registers.
- Pilot metrics.
- Stop and scale criteria.

## Slide 10: Why CAS and Department of Auditing

- Agentic AI needs controls and assurance.
- Agents must be auditable because they may act across systems.
- Auditing expertise strengthens evidence, monitoring and accountability.
- The course turns AI automation into a controlled governance practice.
