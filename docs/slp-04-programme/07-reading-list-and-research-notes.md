# Reading List and Research Notes

## Review Scope

This reading list supports SLP 04: **Agentic HRM and Workflow Automation for Managers**. It uses a rapid scoping approach focused on:

- AI agents and workflow automation.
- Human agency and task suitability.
- HRM service delivery and workflow redesign.
- Permissions, audit trails and human-in-the-loop controls.
- Risk management and responsible AI governance.

## Evidence Synthesis

### Agentic AI Is a Frontier Area

The research base on AI agents is growing quickly but remains less mature than general HR analytics or responsible AI governance. The course should therefore avoid presenting agentic AI as a settled best practice. It should teach readiness, controlled pilots and evidence-based caution.

### Automation and Augmentation Should Be Separated

The strongest curriculum distinction is between automating tasks and augmenting human work. Some HR tasks may be suitable for low-risk assistance, such as routing, retrieval, summarisation or scheduling. High-stakes decisions involving hiring, discipline, promotion or employee rights require stronger human control.

### Human Agency Is Central

Agentic AI can create overreliance and reduce human agency if agents are allowed to act across systems without clear approval. The course therefore makes human-in-the-loop design, escalation and stop criteria central assessment requirements.

### Permissions and Audit Trails Are the Governance Core

The distinctive risk of AI agents is that they may act, not just advise. This means the curriculum must include permissions, prohibited actions, logs, audit trails, identity, monitoring and incident response.

### Auditing Adds a Useful Lens

The Department of Auditing strengthens the course by focusing on controls, evidence, accountability and assurance. Learners should be able to show what the agent did, why it acted, who approved it and what evidence remains.

## Research Limitations

- Agentic AI literature is still emerging.
- Many examples are from prototypes, working papers or vendor-led practice.
- HRM-specific field evidence is limited.
- Course materials should be reviewed before each intake because tools, capabilities and risks are changing quickly.
