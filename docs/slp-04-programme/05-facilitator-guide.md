# Facilitator Guide

## Facilitator Role

The facilitator should help learners evaluate agentic AI with curiosity and caution. The course should avoid both hype and fear. Learners should finish with a practical workflow blueprint that shows where agentic AI may help and how it should be controlled.

## Teaching Principles

- Keep the course non-technical.
- Focus on workflows, permissions and controls.
- Treat agentic AI as emerging and not fully settled.
- Emphasise human agency, auditability and escalation.
- Use fictional or anonymised cases.
- Discourage unsupervised automation in high-stakes HR decisions.
- Require learners to define stop criteria for pilots.

## Suggested Weekly Facilitation Notes

### Week 1

Start with the learner's experience of chatbots, copilots and automation. Explain that agents are different because they may act across steps and tools.

### Week 2

Use examples to compare chatbot, copilot, workflow automation and AI agent. Emphasise that definitions are still evolving.

### Week 3

Run a task triage exercise. Ask learners what should be automated, augmented, redesigned first or avoided.

### Week 4

Teach workflow mapping. Keep maps simple but complete. Steps, actors, data, systems and decisions matter.

### Week 5

Assess readiness. Learners often want to automate too quickly. Ask whether the process is standardised and governed enough.

### Week 6

Focus on exception handling. Good automation design must plan for what happens when the normal process breaks.

### Week 7

Teach human-in-the-loop design concretely. Require learners to name the human role, review action, authority and timing.

### Week 8

Focus on permissions. The more the agent can access and do, the stronger the controls must be.

### Week 9

Build the pilot plan. Push learners toward narrow, reversible pilots.

### Week 10

Discuss vendors and security. Learners should ask where data goes, what logs exist and whether actions can be audited.

### Week 11

Define scale/stop criteria. A pilot that is fast but unsafe should not scale.

### Week 12

Final presentations should defend the pilot recommendation and explain what evidence would be monitored.

## Feedback Guidance

When giving feedback, focus on:

- Is the workflow specific?
- Is agentic automation appropriate?
- Are permissions limited?
- Are human review points concrete?
- Are escalation paths realistic?
- Is audit evidence defined?
- Are success and stop criteria measurable?

## Common Learner Challenges

| Challenge | Facilitator Response |
|---|---|
| Learner automates before mapping | Require current workflow map first |
| Learner gives the agent too much access | Ask what minimum permissions are needed |
| Human oversight is vague | Ask who reviews, when and with what authority |
| Pilot scope is too broad | Narrow to one workflow and one user group |
| Learner ignores failure modes | Require exception and incident scenarios |

## Suggested Discussion Prompts

- What should an HR AI agent never be allowed to do?
- When is automation helpful, and when does it reduce human agency?
- What evidence should be logged for audit?
- Who should own an AI agent in an HR workflow?
- What would make you stop an agentic AI pilot?
