# Assessment Pack and Rubrics

## Assessment Overview

The course uses an applied governance portfolio model. Each formative task builds toward the final Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM.

| Assessment | Weight | Due | Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Formative Task 1 | 10% | Week 3 | Stakeholder and risk map |
| Formative Task 2 | 15% | Week 6 | POPIA and responsible AI compliance checklist |
| Formative Task 3 | 15% | Week 8 | Fairness and bias review memo |
| Final Portfolio | 60% | Week 12 | Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM |

## Formative Task 1: Stakeholder and Risk Map

### Purpose

Learners identify an HR AI use case, affected stakeholders and potential harms.

### Instructions

Select one HRM or employment-related AI use case. Examples include AI-assisted shortlisting, employee monitoring, learning recommendations, performance analytics, onboarding chatbots, workforce planning or employee sentiment analysis.

Submit a 1,000 to 1,200 word stakeholder and risk map covering:

- Use-case description.
- Intended purpose.
- Affected persons.
- Decision points.
- Possible benefits.
- Possible harms.
- Initial risk rating.
- Governance questions that must be answered before use.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Clear use-case framing | 20% |
| Stakeholder and affected-person analysis | 25% |
| Risk and harm identification | 30% |
| Practical governance questions | 15% |
| Clarity and professionalism | 10% |

## Formative Task 2: POPIA and Responsible AI Compliance Checklist

### Purpose

Learners translate POPIA and responsible AI concepts into practical review questions for an HR AI use case.

### Instructions

Create a checklist for your selected use case. The checklist must address:

- Purpose and lawful basis.
- Data minimisation.
- Information quality.
- Openness and employee/candidate notice.
- Security safeguards.
- Automated decision-making risk.
- Human oversight.
- Vendor or third-party responsibilities.
- Recordkeeping.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| POPIA relevance | 25% |
| Responsible AI coverage | 25% |
| Practical checklist design | 20% |
| Vendor and third-party awareness | 15% |
| Clarity and usability | 15% |

## Formative Task 3: Fairness and Bias Review Memo

### Purpose

Learners identify bias risks and propose fairness review controls.

### Instructions

Write a memo to a governance committee or HR executive explaining fairness and bias risks in your selected use case. Include:

- Bias sources.
- Data quality concerns.
- Groups potentially affected.
- Proxy-variable concerns.
- Explainability and contestability issues.
- Recommended fairness checks.
- Review and appeal process.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Bias source analysis | 25% |
| Data quality and proxy-variable awareness | 20% |
| Fairness controls | 25% |
| Contestability and human review | 20% |
| Professional memo style | 10% |

## Final Portfolio: Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM

### Purpose

Learners integrate the course learning into a governance pack for one HR AI use case.

### Required Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Use-case description and business rationale.
3. Stakeholder and affected-person map.
4. Risk classification and risk register.
5. POPIA and employee data review.
6. AI impact assessment.
7. Bias and fairness review.
8. Human oversight and escalation model.
9. Vendor, procurement or third-party review.
10. Governance control matrix.
11. Monitoring, audit evidence and incident-response plan.
12. Recommendation: approve, approve with controls, defer, or reject.

Recommended length: 4,000 to 5,500 words.

### Final Portfolio Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Use-case framing and business rationale | 10% |
| Stakeholder and harm analysis | 10% |
| POPIA and data protection review | 15% |
| Responsible AI risk assessment | 15% |
| Bias, fairness and contestability controls | 15% |
| Governance control matrix and accountability model | 15% |
| Monitoring, audit evidence and incident response | 10% |
| Professional structure and recommendation quality | 10% |

## AI Use in Assessment

Learners may use AI tools for brainstorming, drafting or checking structure, but they must:

- Disclose how AI was used.
- Avoid uploading personal, candidate, employee or confidential organisational information.
- Verify AI-generated content against course materials and sources.
- Take responsibility for all final recommendations.
