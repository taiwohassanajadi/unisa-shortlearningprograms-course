# Reading List and Research Notes

## Review Scope

This reading list supports SLP 02: **Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance**. It uses a rapid scoping approach focused on:

- AI governance and risk management.
- HRM as a high-stakes AI domain.
- POPIA and employee personal information.
- Bias, discrimination and fairness auditing.
- Vendor and third-party AI risk.
- Human oversight, documentation and audit evidence.

## Required Readings

1. NIST. *AI Risk Management Framework.*  
   URL: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework  
   Course use: Weeks 2, 5, 9, 10 and 11. NIST AI RMF provides a risk management structure for mapping, measuring, managing and governing AI risks.

2. NIST. *Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework: Generative Artificial Intelligence Profile.*  
   URL: https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework/generative-artificial-intelligence  
   Course use: Weeks 5, 9 and 11. The GenAI profile helps learners identify risks specific to generative AI systems.

3. Information Regulator South Africa. POPIA resources.  
   URL: https://inforegulator.org.za/acts/  
   Course use: Weeks 4, 6 and 9. Provides the South African legal foundation for personal information governance.

4. Government of South Africa. *Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013.*  
   URL: https://www.gov.za/sites/default/files/gcis_document/201409/3706726-11act4of2013protectionofpersonalinforcorrect.pdf  
   Course use: Week 4. Key provisions include lawful processing conditions, special personal information and automated decision-making.

5. ISO. *ISO/IEC 42001:2023 Artificial intelligence management system.*  
   URL: https://www.iso.org/standard/42001  
   Course use: Weeks 5 and 10. Provides an organisational management-system lens for AI governance.

6. OECD. *AI Principles.*  
   URL: https://oecd.ai/en/ai-principles  
   Course use: Week 2. Provides human-centred, accountable and transparent AI principles.

7. European Commission. *AI Act.*  
   URL: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai  
   Course use: Week 5. Used as an international reference point for risk-based AI governance.

8. Tambe, P., Cappelli, P., & Yakubovich, V. *Artificial Intelligence in Human Resources Management: Challenges and a Path Forward.*  
   URL: https://doi.org/10.1177/0008125619867910  
   Course use: Weeks 1, 3 and 10. Explains the distinctive challenges of AI in HRM.

9. Kochling, A., & Wehner, M. C. *Discriminated by an algorithm.*  
   URL: https://doi.org/10.1007/s40685-020-00134-w  
   Course use: Weeks 7 and 8. Supports bias and discrimination discussion in recruitment and HR development.

10. Fabris, A., et al. *Fairness and Bias in Algorithmic Hiring.*  
    URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.13933  
    Course use: Weeks 7 and 8. Supports the fairness audit and algorithmic hiring case work.

## Evidence Synthesis

### HR AI Requires Heightened Governance

HR AI systems can affect access to work, training, promotion, monitoring, performance judgments and workplace dignity. This makes HRM a high-stakes domain. The course therefore treats governance as a core management skill, not a compliance afterthought.

### POPIA Creates Practical Duties for Employee and Candidate Data

POPIA is central because HR AI systems often process personal information. Learners need to understand purpose, minimality, information quality, openness, security safeguards, special personal information and automated decision-making in practical terms.

### International Frameworks Help Operationalise Responsible AI

NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001 and OECD principles offer complementary lenses. NIST supports risk processes, ISO supports management-system governance and OECD supports human-centred policy principles. The EU AI Act is not South African law, but it is useful as an international reference point because employment-related AI is treated as high-risk in many cases.

### Bias and Fairness Require Evidence, Not Slogans

Bias can arise from historical data, proxy variables, poor labels, unrepresentative datasets, system design or human interpretation. Fairness auditing should document evidence, identify affected groups, recommend controls and create review or appeal processes.

### Auditing Adds a Distinctive Institutional Lens

The Department of Auditing can add value by framing HR AI governance around controls, evidence, monitoring, assurance, incident response and accountability.

## Research Limitations

- AI-specific South African employment governance is still developing.
- Many HR AI studies focus on recruitment rather than the full employee lifecycle.
- Vendor systems are often opaque, which limits independent evaluation.
- Legal content should be reviewed before each intake to reflect current regulatory updates.
