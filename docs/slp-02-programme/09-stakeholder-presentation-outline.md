# Stakeholder Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

**Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance**

Subtitle: Proposed Short Learning Programme under CAS, Department of Auditing, Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, and Centre for Lifelong Learning.

## Slide 2: Why This Programme, Why Now

- AI is entering recruitment, screening, monitoring, learning and workforce planning.
- HR AI systems can affect employment opportunity and worker dignity.
- Organisations need practical governance, not only AI awareness.
- CAS and the Department of Auditing can contribute a strong control, assurance and accountability lens.

## Slide 3: The Problem

Poorly governed HR AI can:

- Amplify bias.
- Violate privacy.
- Produce opaque decisions.
- Weaken employee trust.
- Create legal and reputational risk.
- Leave organisations without audit evidence.

## Slide 4: Target Learners

- HR leaders.
- Departmental heads.
- HR compliance practitioners.
- Internal auditors.
- Public sector managers.
- Data protection officers.
- Labour relations practitioners.
- Risk managers.
- Consultants and postgraduate learners.

## Slide 5: Programme Promise

Learners will leave with a practical **Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM** that can be used to review an HR AI use case before implementation.

## Slide 6: Programme Structure

- Week 1 to 3: Responsible AI principles and HRM risk.
- Week 4 to 6: POPIA, standards and vendor accountability.
- Week 7 to 8: Bias, discrimination and fairness auditing.
- Week 9 to 11: Impact assessment, controls, monitoring and incident response.
- Week 12: Final governance pack presentation.

## Slide 7: Four Study Units

1. Responsible AI Principles for HRM.
2. Legal, Regulatory and Standards Context.
3. Bias, Discrimination and Fairness Auditing.
4. AI Governance Operating Model for HRM.

## Slide 8: Assessment Model

- Stakeholder and risk map: 10%.
- POPIA and responsible AI checklist: 15%.
- Fairness and bias review memo: 15%.
- Final governance pack: 60%.

## Slide 9: Responsible AI and POPIA

The programme integrates:

- POPIA and employee personal information.
- Automated decision-making concerns.
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework.
- ISO/IEC 42001.
- OECD AI Principles.
- EU AI Act as an international reference point.
- Human oversight and contestability.

## Slide 10: Why CAS and Department of Auditing

- AI governance requires control thinking.
- HR AI systems need evidence, monitoring and assurance.
- Auditing expertise strengthens risk registers, control matrices and review cycles.
- The programme connects AI ethics with practical accountability.

This SLP helps organisations move from AI adoption to accountable, auditable and human-centred AI governance in HRM.
