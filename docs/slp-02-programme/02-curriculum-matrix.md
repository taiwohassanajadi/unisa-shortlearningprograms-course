# Curriculum and Weekly Learning Matrix

## Weekly Structure

The course is designed for 12 weeks. Each week includes guided study, applied governance practice, discussion and contribution to the final governance pack.

| Week | Study Unit | Topic | Learning Outcomes | Learning Activities | Assessment Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AHRM02A | Orientation: why AI governance matters in HRM | Explain the course purpose and identify HR AI governance risks | Baseline AI governance self-audit, discussion of HR AI use cases | Portfolio context note |
| 2 | AHRM02A | Responsible AI principles | Explain fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, robustness and human oversight | Principle-to-practice mapping, stakeholder discussion | Short reflection |
| 3 | AHRM02A | HRM as a high-stakes AI domain | Identify affected persons and high-risk employment contexts | Use-case triage, stakeholder harm mapping | Formative task 1 submission |
| 4 | AHRM02B | POPIA and employee personal information | Apply POPIA concepts to HR data and automated decisions | POPIA checklist exercise, employee data scenario | Formative task 2 preparation |
| 5 | AHRM02B | AI risk frameworks and standards | Compare NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001, OECD principles and EU AI Act reference points | Framework comparison, governance vocabulary map | Compliance checklist draft |
| 6 | AHRM02B | Vendor, procurement and third-party AI tools | Assess HR AI vendors and supplier accountability | Vendor questionnaire, data flow review | Formative task 2 submission |
| 7 | AHRM02C | Bias, discrimination and data quality | Identify bias sources in recruitment, development and monitoring systems | Bias source mapping, data quality review | Formative task 3 preparation |
| 8 | AHRM02C | Fairness auditing and explainability | Design fairness checks, documentation and contestability practices | Fairness review memo workshop | Formative task 3 submission |
| 9 | AHRM02D | AI impact assessment for HRM | Build an HR AI impact assessment | AI impact assessment clinic | Portfolio draft |
| 10 | AHRM02D | Governance operating model and control matrix | Define roles, controls, evidence and review cycles | RACI and control matrix design | Portfolio draft |
| 11 | AHRM02D | Monitoring, incidents and audit evidence | Design monitoring, audit trail and incident-response processes | Incident simulation, audit evidence checklist | Portfolio draft review |
| 12 | Capstone | Final governance pack presentation | Present a defensible HR AI governance pack | Peer presentation, final reflection | Final portfolio submission |

## Study Unit Outcomes

### AHRM02A - Responsible AI Principles for HRM

Learners should be able to:

- Explain responsible AI principles in plain workplace language.
- Distinguish low-risk AI assistance from high-stakes employment decisions.
- Identify affected persons and possible harms.
- Explain why HRM requires transparency, human oversight and accountability.

### AHRM02B - Legal, Regulatory and Standards Context

Learners should be able to:

- Apply POPIA concepts to employee, candidate and workforce data.
- Explain why automated decision-making requires careful governance.
- Compare major AI governance frameworks and standards.
- Draft vendor and procurement questions for HR AI systems.

### AHRM02C - Bias, Discrimination and Fairness Auditing

Learners should be able to:

- Identify possible bias sources in HR AI systems.
- Evaluate data quality, representativeness and proxy variables.
- Recommend fairness checks and documentation practices.
- Design appeal, review and contestability processes.

### AHRM02D - AI Governance Operating Model for HRM

Learners should be able to:

- Create an AI impact assessment.
- Draft a governance control matrix.
- Define roles for HR, IT, legal, audit, procurement, management and worker representatives.
- Design monitoring, review and incident-response practices.

## Workload Estimate

Recommended weekly workload:

- Guided reading: 2 hours.
- Learning material and recorded lecture: 1 hour.
- Practical governance activity: 2 hours.
- Discussion or peer review: 1 hour.
- Portfolio development: 1 to 2 hours.

Estimated total workload: 84 to 96 hours.
