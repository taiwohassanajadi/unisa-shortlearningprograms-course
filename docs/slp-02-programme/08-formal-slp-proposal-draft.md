# Formal SLP Proposal Draft

## Programme Title

**Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance**

## Host College, Department and Centres

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Motivation

AI-enabled HRM systems are increasingly used to support recruitment, screening, onboarding, learning, employee monitoring, workforce planning and performance-related decisions. These systems can create value, but they also introduce risks relating to privacy, bias, transparency, accountability, employee trust and compliance.

The University of South Africa is well positioned to offer a practical SLP that helps managers, HR leaders, auditors, compliance professionals and postgraduate learners govern HR AI systems responsibly. The Department of Auditing provides a strong institutional lens for controls, assurance, documentation and risk evidence.

## Target Group

The programme is aimed at:

- HR leaders.
- Departmental heads.
- HR compliance practitioners.
- Internal auditors.
- Public sector managers.
- Data protection officers.
- Labour relations practitioners.
- Risk managers.
- Consultants.
- Postgraduate learners and researchers.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to identify, assess, govern and monitor ethical, legal and operational risks associated with AI-enabled HRM systems.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

Learners who complete the programme should be able to:

1. Explain responsible AI principles and why HRM use cases require heightened governance.
2. Identify high-risk HR AI use cases, affected stakeholders and possible harms.
3. Apply POPIA, responsible AI and AI risk management concepts to employment scenarios.
4. Create an AI risk register and impact assessment for an HRM use case.
5. Recommend governance controls, human oversight, audit evidence and monitoring practices for HR AI systems.

## Programme Duration

12 weeks.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Language Medium

English.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have basic digital literacy and an interest in HRM, auditing, management, compliance, risk, governance, data protection or labour relations. Coding knowledge is not required.

## Programme Content

### Module 1: Responsible AI Principles for HRM

Purpose: To introduce responsible AI principles and explain why HRM is a high-stakes domain.

Areas covered:

- Fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, robustness and human oversight.
- HRM and employment opportunity.
- Worker dignity and employee trust.
- Stakeholder and harm mapping.

### Module 2: Legal, Regulatory and Standards Context

Purpose: To teach learners how to use POPIA and international AI governance frameworks in practical HRM review.

Areas covered:

- POPIA and employee personal information.
- Automated decision-making.
- NIST AI RMF.
- ISO/IEC 42001.
- OECD AI Principles.
- EU AI Act as an international reference point.
- Vendor and third-party accountability.

### Module 3: Bias, Discrimination and Fairness Auditing

Purpose: To teach learners how to identify, document and mitigate fairness risks.

Areas covered:

- Bias sources.
- Data quality and representativeness.
- Proxy variables.
- Fairness testing.
- Explainability and contestability.
- Appeal and review processes.

### Module 4: AI Governance Operating Model for HRM

Purpose: To enable learners to design governance controls, oversight and audit evidence for HR AI systems.

Areas covered:

- AI impact assessment.
- Risk register.
- Governance control matrix.
- Roles and responsibilities.
- Monitoring and incident response.
- Audit evidence and review cycles.

## Assessment

The programme uses a portfolio-based assessment model:

- Formative task 1: Stakeholder and risk map, 10%.
- Formative task 2: POPIA and responsible AI compliance checklist, 15%.
- Formative task 3: Fairness and bias review memo, 15%.
- Final portfolio: Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM, 60%.

## Final Portfolio

The final portfolio requires learners to prepare a governance pack for one HR AI use case. It includes:

- Use-case description.
- Stakeholder and affected-person map.
- Risk register.
- POPIA and data protection review.
- AI impact assessment.
- Bias and fairness review.
- Human oversight model.
- Governance control matrix.
- Monitoring and incident-response plan.
- Final recommendation.

## Resource Requirements

Recommended resources:

- Learning management system access.
- Reading pack.
- Case studies and worksheets.
- Facilitator with governance, HRM, auditing, data protection or responsible AI knowledge.
- Reviewer support for POPIA and AI governance content.

## Quality Assurance

Recommended review before launch:

- Academic reviewer from CAS / Department of Auditing.
- HRM practitioner reviewer.
- POPIA-informed reviewer.
- AI governance or information systems reviewer.
- Learning design reviewer.

## Evidence Base

The programme is informed by NIST AI RMF, NIST GenAI Profile, ISO/IEC 42001, POPIA, OECD AI Principles, EU AI Act reference materials and peer-reviewed HR AI fairness literature.
