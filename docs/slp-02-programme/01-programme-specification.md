# Programme Specification

## Programme Title

**Responsible AI Governance in HRM: Ethics, Risk and Compliance**

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Academic Home

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HR leaders, departmental heads, HR compliance practitioners, internal auditors, public sector managers, data protection officers, labour relations practitioners, risk managers, consultants, postgraduate learners and researchers concerned with responsible AI use in employment contexts.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have:

- Basic digital literacy.
- Interest or experience in HRM, management, auditing, compliance, risk, governance, data protection or labour relations.
- Ability to analyse workplace policies, processes and risks.
- No coding requirement.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to identify, assess, govern and monitor ethical, legal, operational and audit-related risks associated with AI-enabled HRM and people-management systems.

## Problem Statement

AI is increasingly used in recruitment, screening, onboarding, learning, performance management, employee monitoring, workforce planning and employee communication. These use cases affect privacy, fairness, transparency, worker dignity and access to opportunity. Poor governance can amplify discrimination, violate POPIA, weaken trust and expose organisations to legal, reputational and operational risk.

This SLP gives learners a practical governance toolkit for deciding whether an HR AI use case should proceed, what controls are needed, who is accountable and how evidence of responsible governance should be documented.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

On successful completion, learners should be able to:

1. Explain responsible AI principles and why HRM use cases require heightened governance.
2. Identify high-risk HR AI use cases, affected stakeholders and possible harms.
3. Apply POPIA, responsible AI and AI risk management concepts to employment scenarios.
4. Create an AI risk register and impact assessment for an HRM use case.
5. Recommend governance controls, human oversight, audit evidence and monitoring practices for HR AI systems.

## Programme Structure

The programme has four study units and one capstone portfolio:

- **AHRM02A:** Responsible AI Principles for HRM
- **AHRM02B:** Legal, Regulatory and Standards Context
- **AHRM02C:** Bias, Discrimination and Fairness Auditing
- **AHRM02D:** AI Governance Operating Model for HRM
- **Capstone:** Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM

## Assessment Structure

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

## Capstone Output

Learners produce a **Responsible AI Governance Pack for HRM** containing:

- Use-case description.
- Stakeholder and affected-person map.
- Risk classification.
- POPIA and data protection review.
- AI impact assessment.
- Bias and fairness review.
- Human oversight and escalation model.
- Governance control matrix.
- Monitoring, audit and incident-response plan.

## Graduate Attributes

The programme supports:

- Ethical and accountable leadership.
- Audit-minded risk assessment.
- Responsible digital capability.
- Evidence-based governance.
- Human-centred employment practice.
- Practical compliance communication.
