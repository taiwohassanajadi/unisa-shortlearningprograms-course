# Assessment Pack and Rubrics

## Assessment Overview

The course uses an applied portfolio model. Each formative task builds toward the final portfolio.

| Assessment | Weight | Due | Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Formative Task 1 | 10% | Week 3 | AI use-case map |
| Formative Task 2 | 15% | Week 6 | Prompt workflow and quality checks |
| Formative Task 3 | 15% | Week 8 | Work redesign map |
| Final Portfolio | 60% | Week 12 | Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook |

## Formative Task 1: AI Use-Case Map

### Purpose

Learners identify one workplace or HRM process where generative AI could assist and assess whether the use case is suitable.

### Instructions

Select one process from your department, team, project or HR context. Examples include onboarding, weekly reporting, meeting preparation, role clarification, learning support, team communication or policy drafting.

Submit a 1,000 to 1,200 word use-case map covering:

- Process name.
- Current pain point.
- People affected.
- Current workflow summary.
- Possible AI assistance.
- Risks and safeguards.
- Initial success measures.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Clear process and problem framing | 25% |
| Suitable AI use-case reasoning | 25% |
| Stakeholder and risk awareness | 25% |
| Practical success measures | 15% |
| Clarity and professionalism | 10% |

## Formative Task 2: Prompt Workflow and Quality Checks

### Purpose

Learners demonstrate practical LLM use for a workplace task while applying output checks.

### Instructions

Build a prompt workflow for one managerial or HRM-related task. Submit:

- The task and context.
- Three prompt versions showing improvement.
- The AI output or simulated output.
- A quality checklist.
- A fairness, confidentiality and accuracy review.
- A final revised output.

Recommended length: 1,200 to 1,500 words.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Prompt clarity and structure | 25% |
| Iterative improvement | 20% |
| Quality assurance method | 25% |
| Ethical and confidentiality awareness | 20% |
| Presentation and reflection | 10% |

## Formative Task 3: Work Redesign Map

### Purpose

Learners redesign a current process by adding AI support, human review and governance controls.

### Instructions

Submit a current-state and future-state workflow map. The map may be visual or table-based. Include:

- Current workflow steps.
- Pain points.
- AI support points.
- Human decision points.
- Escalation points.
- Risks and mitigation.
- Expected improvement.

Recommended length: 1,000 to 1,500 words plus diagram or table.

### Marking Criteria

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Current workflow clarity | 20% |
| Quality of AI-assisted future workflow | 25% |
| Human-in-the-loop design | 25% |
| Risk controls | 20% |
| Practicality and clarity | 10% |

## Final Portfolio: Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook

### Purpose

Learners integrate the course learning into a practical playbook for one selected workplace or HRM process.

### Required Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Workplace context.
3. Selected problem and rationale.
4. Current workflow map.
5. AI-assisted future workflow.
6. Prompt workflow and quality checks.
7. Responsible AI and POPIA risk review.
8. Human review and escalation model.
9. Implementation plan.
10. Measurement plan.
11. Reflection on limitations and ethical considerations.

Recommended length: 3,500 to 5,000 words.

### Final Portfolio Rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Problem framing and workplace relevance | 10% |
| Evidence-informed understanding of LLM use | 10% |
| Quality of workflow redesign | 15% |
| Prompt workflow and quality assurance | 15% |
| Responsible AI, POPIA and confidentiality controls | 15% |
| Human oversight and escalation design | 10% |
| Implementation and measurement plan | 15% |
| Professional structure and communication | 10% |

## AI Use in Assessment

Learners may use AI tools for drafting, brainstorming and review, but they must:

- Disclose how AI was used.
- Protect personal and confidential information.
- Verify outputs.
- Take responsibility for the final submission.
- Avoid submitting AI-generated work without critical review and contextualisation.
