# Case Study and Worksheet Pack

## Case Study 1: Departmental Onboarding

### Scenario

A university department has hired three new administrative staff members. The onboarding process is inconsistent. Some new staff receive detailed guidance, while others rely on informal explanations from colleagues. The departmental head wants to use an LLM to improve onboarding communication and role clarity.

### Learner Task

Design an AI-assisted onboarding workflow that:

- Drafts a welcome message.
- Creates a first-week checklist.
- Summarises role expectations.
- Protects employee privacy.
- Requires human review before anything is sent.

### Discussion Questions

- What information should not be entered into the AI tool?
- Who should approve the onboarding material?
- How will the department know whether onboarding improved?

## Case Study 2: Team Reporting

### Scenario

A project lead spends several hours each week turning team updates into a management report. The updates arrive in inconsistent formats. The lead wants to use AI to structure the report and highlight risks.

### Learner Task

Create a prompt workflow for weekly report preparation. Include:

- Input format.
- AI task.
- Output format.
- Accuracy checks.
- Human review points.
- Metrics for success.

### Discussion Questions

- What happens if the AI misses a critical risk?
- What source documents should be checked?
- How can the report remain useful without becoming generic?

## Case Study 3: Performance Conversation Preparation

### Scenario

A line manager wants to prepare for a performance conversation with an employee whose work quality has declined. The manager wants to use AI to prepare talking points.

### Learner Task

Design a safe AI use approach for preparing the conversation. The approach must:

- Avoid entering personal employee details into a public tool.
- Focus on fair, respectful communication.
- Include evidence review.
- Avoid outsourcing the actual management judgment to AI.

### Discussion Questions

- What should remain human-led?
- What evidence should the manager review before the conversation?
- How could AI wording create unintended harm?
