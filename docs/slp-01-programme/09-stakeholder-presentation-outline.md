# Stakeholder Presentation Outline

## Slide 1: Title

**AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**

Subtitle: Proposed Short Learning Programme under CAS, Department of Auditing, Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, and Centre for Lifelong Learning.

## Slide 2: Why This Programme, Why Now

- Generative AI is already being used informally in workplace tasks.
- Managers need more than prompting skills.
- HRM and people-management tasks create confidentiality, fairness and accountability risks.
- UNISA can position the SLP as practical, responsible and multidisciplinary.

## Slide 3: The Problem

Current AI use in workplaces is often:

- Informal.
- Unchecked.
- Disconnected from POPIA and responsible AI.
- Focused on outputs rather than redesigned workflows.
- Weak on human oversight and measurement.

## Slide 4: Target Learners

- Departmental heads.
- Team leads.
- Line managers.
- Supervisors.
- Project and operations leads.
- HR and OD practitioners.
- Consultants.
- Postgraduate learners.

## Slide 5: Programme Promise

Learners will leave with a practical **Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook** for a selected workplace or HRM process.

## Slide 6: Programme Structure

- Week 1 to 3: AI, LLMs and changing work.
- Week 4 to 6: Practical LLM use and quality checks.
- Week 7 to 8: Workflow redesign and human oversight.
- Week 9 to 11: Responsible implementation and measurement.
- Week 12: Final portfolio presentation.

## Slide 7: Four Study Units

1. Generative AI, LLMs and the Changing Workplace.
2. Practical LLM Use for Departmental Heads, Team Leads and HR Practitioners.
3. Work Redesign and Human-in-the-Loop HRM.
4. Responsible Implementation, Governance and Measurement.

## Slide 8: Assessment Model

- AI use-case map: 10%.
- Prompt workflow and quality checks: 15%.
- Work redesign map: 15%.
- Final playbook: 60%.

## Slide 9: Responsible AI and Governance

The programme integrates:

- POPIA and confidentiality.
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework.
- Human review and escalation.
- Fairness and bias awareness.
- AI use disclosure.
- Quality assurance.

## Slide 10: Why CAS and Department of Auditing

- Strong alignment with risk, governance, controls, assurance and evidence.
- AI use in organisations requires audit-minded thinking.
- The Department of Auditing can contribute a strong control and accountability lens.
- The Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies provides multidisciplinary AI positioning.
- The Centre for Lifelong Learning supports professional upskilling.

## Slide 11: Key Deliverables Already Drafted

- Programme specification.
- 12-week curriculum matrix.
- Learner study guide.
- Assessment pack and rubrics.
- Facilitator guide.
- Case study and worksheet pack.
- Reading list and research notes.

## Slide 12: Approval and Development Pathway

1. Academic and practitioner review.
2. Learning design review.
3. POPIA and responsible AI review.
4. Pilot cohort.
5. Learner feedback and refinement.
6. Full SLP launch.

## Slide 13: Closing Message

This SLP helps managers move from AI experimentation to responsible workplace transformation.
