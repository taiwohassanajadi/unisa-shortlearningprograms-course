# Reading List and Research Notes

## Review Scope

This reading list supports SLP 01: **AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**. It uses a rapid scoping approach focused on:

- Generative AI and workplace productivity.
- AI in HRM.
- Work redesign and task augmentation.
- Responsible AI and risk management.
- Prompting and quality assurance.
- South African data protection relevance.

## Evidence Synthesis

### AI Can Improve Some Work, But Effects Depend on Context

Generative AI can support productivity in text-heavy work, but the evidence does not justify blanket claims that AI automatically improves every job. The course therefore requires learners to define the task, the success criteria and the review method before adopting AI.

### HRM and People-Management Tasks Need Extra Care

HRM tasks affect opportunity, dignity, privacy and trust. Even when the learner is not an HR manager, people-management tasks such as coaching, feedback, onboarding and role design can affect employees. The programme therefore integrates fairness, confidentiality and human accountability throughout the curriculum.

### Prompting Is Necessary But Not Sufficient

Prompting improves AI outputs, but responsible workplace use also needs quality assurance, source checking, role clarity, human review and governance. This is why the course moves from prompt design to workflow redesign.

### Work Redesign Is the Core Educational Contribution

The SLP positions AI as a redesign challenge. Learners are assessed on their ability to redesign a workflow with appropriate AI assistance, not on producing impressive AI-generated text.

## Research Limitations

- The generative AI evidence base is still developing.
- Many studies are from non-African contexts.
- HR AI research often focuses on recruitment, while this course also covers everyday management work.
- Legal and policy requirements should be checked before each course intake.
