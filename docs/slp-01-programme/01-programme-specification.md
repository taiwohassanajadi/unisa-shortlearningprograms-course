# Programme Specification

## Programme Title

**AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Academic Home

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at departmental heads, team leads, line managers, supervisors, project leads, operations leads, HR practitioners, HR business partners, organisational development practitioners, consultants and postgraduate learners who need to use generative AI responsibly in everyday workplace and people-management contexts.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have:

- Basic digital literacy.
- Experience or interest in managing people, teams, projects, operations or HR-related processes.
- Access to a computer, internet connection and at least one approved AI tool or simulated AI activity.
- No coding requirement.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to evaluate, use and govern generative AI tools in managerial work and HRM-related practice. The programme emphasises work redesign, human oversight, responsible AI use, employee trust, productivity and measurable workplace value.

## Problem Statement

Generative AI tools are entering everyday workplace practice faster than many organisations can govern them. Managers and team leads use LLMs for writing, summarising, planning, policy drafting, onboarding, learning design and performance communication. The risk is that these uses often remain informal and disconnected from POPIA, fairness, confidentiality, quality assurance and work redesign.

This SLP addresses that gap by teaching learners to move from isolated prompting to responsible workflow redesign.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

On successful completion, learners should be able to:

1. Explain how LLMs affect workplace tasks, managerial work, HRM processes and employee experience.
2. Identify workplace and HRM use cases where generative AI can assist, augment or create risk.
3. Design prompt workflows for common managerial and HRM-related tasks while protecting confidentiality, fairness and quality.
4. Map a current workplace workflow and redesign it for responsible AI assistance.
5. Develop a practical AI + HRM work redesign playbook for use in an organisation or case setting.

## Programme Structure

The programme has four study units and one capstone portfolio:

- **AHRM01A:** Generative AI, LLMs and the Changing Workplace
- **AHRM01B:** Practical LLM Use for Departmental Heads, Team Leads and HR Practitioners
- **AHRM01C:** Work Redesign and Human-in-the-Loop HRM
- **AHRM01D:** Responsible Implementation, Governance and Measurement
- **Capstone:** Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook

## Assessment Structure

- Formative task 1: 10%
- Formative task 2: 15%
- Formative task 3: 15%
- Final applied portfolio: 60%

## Capstone Output

Learners produce a **Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook** containing:

- Selected workplace or HRM problem.
- Current workflow map.
- AI-assisted future workflow.
- Prompt workflow and quality checks.
- Risk controls.
- Human review points.
- POPIA and confidentiality considerations.
- Implementation and measurement plan.

## Graduate Attributes

The programme supports:

- Responsible digital capability.
- Ethical decision-making.
- Evidence-informed management.
- Workplace problem-solving.
- Inclusive and human-centred work redesign.
- Practical communication for organisational change.
