# Formal SLP Proposal Draft

## Programme Title

**AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**

## Host College, Department and Centres

- **College:** College of Accounting Sciences (CAS)
- **Department:** Department of Auditing
- **Institute / collaboration platform:** Centre for Artificial Intelligence & Multidisciplinary Innovation Studies, University of South Africa
- **Lifelong learning collaboration:** Centre for Lifelong Learning

## Motivation

Generative AI is entering everyday managerial and HRM-related work through tools such as ChatGPT, Claude and similar large language model systems. Departmental heads, team leads, line managers, supervisors, project leads, operations leads and HR practitioners are already using these tools for planning, communication, reporting, onboarding, policy drafting, learning support and performance conversation preparation.

The major institutional need is not only tool familiarity. Learners need structured capability to decide when AI should be used, how outputs should be checked, how work should be redesigned and how employee trust, confidentiality, POPIA and fairness should be protected.

This SLP responds to that need by providing a practical, non-technical programme focused on responsible generative AI use in workplace and people-management contexts.

## Target Group

The programme is aimed at:

- Departmental heads.
- Team leads.
- Line managers.
- Supervisors.
- Project leads.
- Operations leads.
- HR practitioners and HR business partners.
- Organisational development practitioners.
- Consultants.
- Postgraduate learners in management, auditing, HRM, public administration, organisational studies and related fields.

## Programme Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach learners how to evaluate, use and govern generative AI tools in managerial work and HRM-related practice, with emphasis on work redesign, productivity, human oversight, responsible AI use and employee experience.

## Exit-Level Outcomes

Learners who complete the programme should be able to:

1. Explain how LLMs affect workplace tasks, managerial work, HRM processes and employee experience.
2. Identify workplace and HRM use cases where generative AI can assist, augment or create risk.
3. Design prompt workflows for common managerial and HRM-related tasks while protecting confidentiality, fairness and quality.
4. Map a current workplace workflow and redesign it for responsible AI assistance.
5. Develop a practical AI + HRM work redesign playbook for a selected organisation, department or case setting.

## Programme Duration

12 weeks.

## Delivery Mode

Online or blended distance learning.

## Language Medium

English.

## Admission Assumptions

Learners should have basic digital literacy and an interest in workplace leadership, people management, HRM or organisational improvement. Coding knowledge is not required.

## Programme Content

### Module 1: Generative AI, LLMs and the Changing Workplace

Purpose: To introduce learners to generative AI, LLMs, task transformation and the managerial implications of AI-enabled work.

Areas covered:

- What LLMs are and what they are not.
- ChatGPT, Claude and similar systems as workplace assistants.
- AI as automation, augmentation and capability-building.
- Labour-market evidence on AI, jobs and skills.
- Workplace and HRM tasks affected by LLMs.

### Module 2: Practical LLM Use for Departmental Heads, Team Leads and HR Practitioners

Purpose: To build practical skills in prompt design and AI-assisted workplace output development.

Areas covered:

- Prompt design for everyday workplace tasks.
- Planning, reporting and communication.
- Role clarification and onboarding.
- Coaching preparation and learning design.
- Quality assurance and output checking.

### Module 3: Work Redesign and Human-in-the-Loop HRM

Purpose: To teach learners how to redesign workflows with responsible AI support and human accountability.

Areas covered:

- Workflow mapping.
- Task triage.
- Human review and escalation.
- Employee trust and acceptance.
- Avoiding deskilling, overreliance and hidden bias.

### Module 4: Responsible Implementation, Governance and Measurement

Purpose: To enable learners to implement AI-enabled work redesign responsibly and measurably.

Areas covered:

- POPIA and confidentiality.
- Responsible AI guardrails.
- Team-level AI use rules.
- Low-risk pilot design.
- Metrics for quality, time, employee experience, risk and learning.

## Assessment

The programme uses a portfolio-based assessment model:

- Formative task 1: AI use-case map, 10%.
- Formative task 2: Prompt workflow and quality checks, 15%.
- Formative task 3: Work redesign map, 15%.
- Final portfolio: Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook, 60%.

## Final Portfolio

The final portfolio requires learners to develop a practical playbook for one workplace or HRM process. The playbook includes:

- Problem statement.
- Current workflow.
- AI-assisted future workflow.
- Prompt workflow.
- Quality checks.
- Responsible AI and POPIA risk review.
- Human review and escalation plan.
- Implementation plan.
- Measurement plan.

## Evidence Base

The programme is informed by international labour-market research, responsible AI frameworks, HRM AI scholarship, POPIA resources and prompt engineering guidance. Key sources include the ILO, WEF, NIST, ISO, Information Regulator South Africa, UNESCO, Tambe et al., Brynjolfsson et al., OpenAI and Anthropic documentation.
