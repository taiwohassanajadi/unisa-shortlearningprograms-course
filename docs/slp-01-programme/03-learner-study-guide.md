# Learner Study Guide

## Welcome

Welcome to **AI + HRM for Managers: From LLM Use to Work Redesign**. This programme is for people who lead work: departmental heads, team leads, line managers, supervisors, project leads, operations leads, HR practitioners and postgraduate learners.

The course is not about becoming a programmer. It is about using generative AI responsibly in the workplace and redesigning work so that people remain accountable, protected and effective.

## How to Use This Study Guide

Each week you should:

1. Read the weekly study notes.
2. Complete the practical activity.
3. Record insights in your learning journal.
4. Add evidence to your final portfolio.
5. Participate in the discussion or peer review activity.

## Core Principle

AI should not be treated as an answer machine. In this programme, AI is treated as a workplace assistant that must be directed, checked and governed by humans.

## Study Unit 1: Generative AI, LLMs and the Changing Workplace

### Key Ideas

Generative AI can draft, summarise, classify, translate, explain, brainstorm and restructure text. LLMs can be useful for managerial work because much of managerial work is language-heavy: memos, reports, policies, plans, meeting notes, feedback, role descriptions and learning material.

However, LLMs can produce false, biased or incomplete outputs. They do not understand your organisation in the same way a responsible manager does. They also do not carry accountability for the consequences of workplace decisions.

### Practical Focus

You will identify where AI is already appearing in your work and where it could create value or risk.

### Reflection Question

Where in your current work do you spend time drafting, summarising, planning or communicating, and what would make AI assistance useful but safe?

## Study Unit 2: Practical LLM Use for Departmental Heads, Team Leads and HR Practitioners

### Key Ideas

Good AI use begins with good instruction. A useful prompt usually includes:

- Role or perspective.
- Task.
- Context.
- Audience.
- Constraints.
- Output format.
- Quality criteria.
- Review request.

A weak prompt asks for a final answer too quickly. A stronger prompt asks the tool to help structure thinking, produce a draft, identify risks or suggest alternatives.

### Example Prompt Pattern

```text
Act as a workplace communication assistant.
Task: Draft a short onboarding message for a new team member.
Context: The person is joining a hybrid team in a public university department.
Audience: The message should be warm, professional and clear.
Constraints: Do not include private employee information. Keep it under 180 words.
Quality check: After the draft, list three risks or missing details I should verify before sending.
```

### Practical Focus

You will build and test prompts for planning, reporting, onboarding, team communication, learning support and performance conversation preparation.

### Reflection Question

What information should never be entered into a public AI tool in your workplace?

## Study Unit 3: Work Redesign and Human-in-the-Loop HRM

### Key Ideas

AI value comes from redesigning how work is done. A manager should ask:

- What task is being supported?
- What is the current pain point?
- What should AI draft, summarise or organise?
- What must a human decide?
- What can go wrong?
- Who checks the output?
- How will success be measured?

Human-in-the-loop design means humans remain responsible at key points. It is especially important when work affects hiring, performance, development, employee wellbeing, disciplinary matters, promotion or access to opportunity.

### Practical Focus

You will map one workplace process and redesign it with clear AI support points and human review points.

### Reflection Question

Where would AI assistance save time in your workflow, and where would it be dangerous to remove human judgment?

## Study Unit 4: Responsible Implementation, Governance and Measurement

### Key Ideas

Responsible AI use requires guardrails. At minimum, a team-level AI use guide should explain:

- Approved and unapproved use cases.
- Data that must not be entered into AI tools.
- Human review requirements.
- How to check accuracy.
- How to handle bias or unfairness concerns.
- When to escalate to HR, legal, IT or management.
- How results will be measured.

POPIA matters because workplace AI use can involve personal information, employee records, candidate information, health data, performance information or other sensitive details.

### Practical Focus

You will create a risk-aware implementation plan for a small AI-enabled work redesign pilot.

### Reflection Question

How will you know whether AI improved the work rather than merely making it faster?

## Final Portfolio

Your final portfolio is a **Responsible LLM Use and HR Work Redesign Playbook**. It should be practical enough that a manager could use it to run a low-risk AI pilot in a real department or team.
