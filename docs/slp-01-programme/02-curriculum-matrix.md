# Curriculum and Weekly Learning Matrix

## Weekly Structure

The course is designed for 12 weeks. Each week includes guided study, a practical activity, a reflective question and contribution to the final portfolio.

| Week | Study Unit | Topic | Learning Outcomes | Learning Activities | Assessment Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | AHRM01A | Orientation: AI, HRM and managerial work | Explain the course purpose and identify workplace AI use cases | Orientation discussion, AI use self-audit, baseline confidence survey | Portfolio context note |
| 2 | AHRM01A | What LLMs can and cannot do | Explain LLM capabilities, limitations and risks | Tool demonstration, limitation analysis, hallucination spotting | Short reflection |
| 3 | AHRM01A | AI, jobs, skills and task redesign | Analyse AI as task augmentation and work redesign | Task inventory, job impact discussion, ILO/WEF reading discussion | Formative task 1 preparation |
| 4 | AHRM01B | Prompting for workplace productivity | Build prompts for planning, reporting and communication | Prompt clinic, before/after output review | Prompt log |
| 5 | AHRM01B | Prompting for people-management tasks | Build prompts for onboarding, role clarity, coaching and learning | Scenario practice, peer review, fairness check | Formative task 2 preparation |
| 6 | AHRM01B | Quality assurance and source checking | Apply output verification and improvement methods | Hallucination check, evidence check, revision workflow | Formative task 2 submission |
| 7 | AHRM01C | Workflow mapping and work redesign | Map a current process and identify AI assistance points | Workflow mapping workshop, task triage | Formative task 3 preparation |
| 8 | AHRM01C | Human-in-the-loop design | Design human review points and escalation controls | Human oversight mapping, accountability discussion | Formative task 3 preparation |
| 9 | AHRM01D | Responsible AI, POPIA and confidentiality | Apply responsible AI and data protection principles | Risk checklist, POPIA scenario discussion | Risk register draft |
| 10 | AHRM01D | Implementation planning and change communication | Plan a small AI-enabled work redesign pilot | Pilot canvas, communication plan | Portfolio draft |
| 11 | AHRM01D | Measurement and evaluation | Define success metrics and review cycles | Metrics workshop, evaluation plan | Portfolio draft review |
| 12 | Capstone | Final portfolio presentation and reflection | Present a responsible AI + HRM work redesign playbook | Peer presentation, final reflection | Final portfolio submission |

## Study Unit Outcomes

### AHRM01A - Generative AI, LLMs and the Changing Workplace

Learners should be able to:

- Define generative AI and LLMs in accessible management language.
- Explain why LLMs are useful for text-heavy managerial work.
- Distinguish task automation, task augmentation and work redesign.
- Discuss labour-market evidence on AI, jobs and skills.

### AHRM01B - Practical LLM Use for Departmental Heads, Team Leads and HR Practitioners

Learners should be able to:

- Write structured prompts for workplace tasks.
- Use prompts for planning, communication, onboarding, learning and coaching scenarios.
- Improve AI outputs through context, examples, constraints and review.
- Apply basic quality checks before using AI-assisted outputs.

### AHRM01C - Work Redesign and Human-in-the-Loop HRM

Learners should be able to:

- Map a current workflow.
- Identify where AI should assist, where humans should decide and where AI should not be used.
- Build human review points into a redesigned workflow.
- Explain how redesign affects productivity, fairness, trust and accountability.

### AHRM01D - Responsible Implementation, Governance and Measurement

Learners should be able to:

- Identify confidentiality, POPIA, fairness and employee trust risks.
- Draft responsible use rules for a team or department.
- Create a low-risk pilot plan.
- Select metrics for quality, time, risk, employee experience and learning.

## Workload Estimate

Recommended weekly workload:

- Guided reading: 2 hours.
- Learning material and recorded lecture: 1 hour.
- Practical activity: 2 hours.
- Reflection or discussion: 1 hour.
- Portfolio development: 1 to 2 hours.

Estimated total workload: 84 to 96 hours.
