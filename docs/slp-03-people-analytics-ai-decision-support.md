# SLP 03: People Analytics and AI Decision Support for HR Managers

## Proposed Qualification Code

To be assigned by UNISA.

## Duration

12 weeks.

## Language Medium

English.

## Target Group

This programme is aimed at HR managers, HR analysts, talent managers, organisational development practitioners, workforce planners, consultants and postgraduate learners who need to use people data and AI-supported analysis for better workplace decisions.

## Problem Being Solved

Many HR teams collect large amounts of employee data but struggle to turn it into trustworthy decisions. Dashboards and AI tools can create a false sense of certainty when measures are weak, data is incomplete, or managers do not understand causality, bias and interpretation.

This SLP teaches people analytics as **decision support**, not dashboard decoration. Learners develop the ability to frame HR questions, choose valid measures, interpret AI-assisted outputs and communicate recommendations responsibly.

## Purpose

The purpose of this programme is to teach skills for using people analytics and AI-assisted decision support in HRM while maintaining evidence quality, fairness, transparency and managerial accountability.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion, learners should be able to:

- Formulate HRM questions that can be answered with people data.
- Distinguish descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics.
- Evaluate the quality, limits and risks of HR data.
- Use AI tools to support analysis while checking assumptions and interpretation.
- Produce a people analytics brief for a workforce decision.

## Study Units

### AHRM03A - Foundations of People Analytics

Content areas:

- People analytics and evidence-based HRM.
- HR metrics versus managerial decisions.
- Data sources: HRIS, surveys, performance data, learning data, recruitment data and work systems.
- Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive and prescriptive analytics.
- Common HR analytics questions: turnover, absenteeism, skills, engagement, recruitment quality and learning impact.

### AHRM03B - Data Quality, Measurement and Interpretation

Content areas:

- Validity, reliability and missing data.
- Small datasets and HRM inference limits.
- Correlation versus causation.
- Segmentation and comparison.
- Bias in people data.
- Communicating uncertainty.

### AHRM03C - AI-Assisted Analysis and Decision Support

Content areas:

- Using LLMs to structure analysis questions.
- AI-assisted summarisation of qualitative employee feedback.
- Dashboard interpretation and narrative reporting.
- Workforce planning scenarios.
- Risk checks for automated recommendations.
- Human decision rights and accountability.

### AHRM03D - Analytics Governance and Action

Content areas:

- POPIA and employee data governance.
- Data minimisation and purpose limitation.
- Ethics of employee monitoring.
- Translating insights into interventions.
- Evaluating whether HR analytics changed outcomes.
- Building a people analytics maturity roadmap.

## Assessment

- **Formative task 1:** HR decision question and analytics framing memo.
- **Formative task 2:** Data quality and risk review.
- **Formative task 3:** AI-assisted analysis interpretation task.
- **Final portfolio:** People analytics decision-support brief for a selected HRM issue.

## Capstone Artefact

Learners produce a **People Analytics Decision Brief** containing:

- HRM problem statement.
- Decision question.
- Data source map.
- Metric definitions.
- Analysis approach.
- Interpretation and uncertainty notes.
- Ethical and POPIA considerations.
- Recommended action and evaluation plan.

## Evidence Base

The literature on HR analytics stresses that value comes from better decisions, not simply more data. AI can assist with pattern recognition, summarisation and scenario generation, but HR decisions require valid measures, contextual judgment, privacy protection and clear accountability.
